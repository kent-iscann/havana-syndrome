# Directed Energy Weapons

**Type:** Concept — Weapons Technology
**Updated:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

Directed energy weapons (DEWs) focus energy (electromagnetic, acoustic, or thermal) toward a target at the speed of light. In the context of Anomalous Health Incidents, DEWs are the leading candidate explanation.

## Types Relevant to AHIs

### Pulsed RF / Microwave
- **Mechanism:** Directed electromagnetic energy pulses penetrate structures and interact with body tissues
- **Fry Effect:** Humans can perceive pulsed RF as sounds (buzzing, chirping, clicking) — discovered by Allan H. Frey in the 1960s
- **Key property:** Can be focused on one room without affecting adjacent rooms (per NASEM)
- **Portability:** By 2026, a backpack-sized device was reportedly acquired by U.S. Homeland Security for testing
- **Status:** Identified as **"most plausible"** mechanism by NASEM (2020)

### Ultrasonic / Acoustic
- **Mechanism:** Directional sound at frequencies above human hearing
- **Effect:** Can cause vestibular disruption, nausea, vertigo, head pressure
- **Status:** Identified as high-probability cause alongside microwaves by Giordano (2017, 2024)

### Laser (Pulsed Infrared)
- **Mechanism:** 2026 hypothesis: pulsed infrared lasers generating acoustic effects in the inner ear (optoacoustic effect)
- **Advantage:** Portable, silent, no heat generation
- **Status:** Proposed by Czech Academy of Sciences fellow (2026)

## Evidence

### Supporting
- NASEM report (2020): Pulsed RF energy "most plausible"
- Giordano forensic evaluation (2017, 2024): Both ultrasonic and pulsed microwave high probability
- DNI Scientific Panel (2022): Pulsed electromagnetic energy and ultrasound "plausible"
- Technologies **"extant, scalable, fieldable, in operational use"** by 2016 (Giordano)
- U.S. Homeland Security acquired backpack-sized pulsed RF device (2026)
- GRU Unit 29155 linked to "non-lethal acoustic weapons" (2024)
- Norwegian scientist reportedly built and tested microwave pulse device on himself (2026)

### Challenging
- NIH studies (2024): No MRI-detectable brain injury biomarkers
- JASON report (2018): Microwave/RF causes "highly unlikely"
- ODNI (2023): No compelling intelligence tying a foreign actor
