# Giordano & DiEuliis — Anomalous Health Incidents and Global Biosecurity

**Source:** National Defense University (NDU), Digital Commons
**Authors:** James Giordano and Diane DiEuliis
**Date:** 2022
**Type:** Research Paper
**Ingested:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

Published by NDU's Center for the Study of Weapons of Mass Destruction (CDTFW), this research paper addressed the **biosecurity and defense implications** of Anomalous Health Incidents, connecting AHIs to broader national security concerns about novel biological weapons and dual-use technologies.

## Key Findings

### Verified Cases
- The approximately **two dozen individuals originally affected in Havana in 2016** are regarded as **verified cases of physical injury**
- ODNI focused on a **subset of priority cases** for which it has not ruled out the possibility of foreign actor involvement

### Technology Assessment
- Several lines of evidence support that **"multinational state-of-the-science-and-technology is sufficiently advanced and developed to produce and operationally employ such devices"**
- Directed energy devices remain a **valid and viable possibility** for causation

### Policy Implications
- AHIs connect to broader **dual-use technology concerns** in biotechnology and directed energy
- The ODNI's 2022 Annual Threat Assessment noted "global shortcomings in preparedness for biosecurity"
- Recent advances in dual-use technology "could enable development of novel biological weapons that complicate detection, attribution, and treatment"
