# GRU Unit 29155 — Evidence Assessment

**Type:** Analysis — Attribution Assessment
**Updated:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

This page assesses the evidence linking **GRU Unit 29155**, a Russian military intelligence special operations unit, to Anomalous Health Incidents worldwide.

### Unit Profile
Unit 29155 is a GRU special operations unit known for:
- **Salisbury poisoning** (2018) — Novichok nerve agent attack
- Other covert operations against Western targets
- **Albert Averyanov** — Son of GRU deputy chief, identified at incident scene

## Weaknesses in Evidence

1. **Correlation ≠ causation** — presence near AHI sites doesn't prove responsibility
2. **Intelligence gaps** — no intercepts, no defector testimony, no weapons seizure
3. **Private denials** — U.S. intelligence collection shows credible adversaries denying involvement
4. **ODNI assessment** — 7 agencies found no compelling evidence tying foreign actor
5. **Alternative explanations** — MPI, environmental factors could explain symptoms without Russian involvement

## Strengths in Evidence

1. **Temporal pattern** — incidents declined after CIA Director Burns warned Russian counterparts (2021-2022)
2. **Multiple corroborating sources** — 60 Minutes, The Insider, Der Spiegel independent investigations
3. **Technical capability** — technologies confirmed extant and fieldable by 2016
4. **Historical precedent** — Moscow Signal establishes pattern of Russian directed energy operations
5. **Unit's known mission set** — covert operations against Western targets aligns with AHI patterns

## Assessment

The evidence is **suggestive but not conclusive**. Unit 29155 has the motive (targeting Western intelligence), opportunity (presence at AHI sites), capability (directed energy weapons), and behavior pattern (covert operations). The strongest single piece of evidence is the **temporal correlation** — incidents declined after Burns' diplomatic warnings.

The weakest point is the **absence of direct intelligence** — no intercepts, no defector, no weapon seized. The ODNI's acknowledgment of "many unanswered questions" reflects this gap.
