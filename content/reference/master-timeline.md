## Overview

A comprehensive chronological timeline of Havana Syndrome and its historical antecedents, from the Moscow Signal (1953) through March 2026. This timeline tracks incidents, investigations, scientific findings, legislative actions, and shifting government assessments.

## 1953–1979: The Moscow Signal Period

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **1953** | The Soviet Union begins beaming microwave radiation at the U.S. Embassy in Moscow — the so-called "Moscow Signal." The signal continues for over two decades. |
| **1959 (July)** | During Nixon's visit to Moscow, Secret Service detects "significant levels of radiation" in VP Richard Nixon's sleeping quarters at Spaso House. The incident is covered up by U.S. officials for 17 years. |
| **1960s–1970s** | U.S. embassy personnel in Moscow experience unexplained health issues. Three U.S. Ambassadors to Moscow (Charles Bohlen, Llewellyn Thompson, Arthur Stoessel) die of cancer or blood disorders at relatively young ages. |
| **1975** | Henry Kissinger tells Soviet Ambassador Anatoly Dobrynin, per declassified transcripts: "That beam you are beaming into our Embassy... Maybe you could turn it off until I get there." |
| **1976–1979** | State Department funds a Johns Hopkins University study on the Moscow Signal. Researchers conclude microwaves are not harmful — a finding that remains controversial. |
| **1978** | Embassy Moscow technical survey by Charles Becker concludes Soviets were "using embassy employees as test subjects for low-level EMR experiments." |

## 1996–2016: Pre-Havana Reports

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **c. 1996** | Possible early AHI cases involving NSA officers are later reported, predating the Havana cluster by two decades. |
| **2007** | Members of George W. Bush's delegation in Germany report dizziness and hearing loss — retrospectively suggested as an early incident. |

## 2016–2018: The Havana Crisis

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **Late 2016 (November)** | A CIA officer working undercover at the U.S. Embassy in Havana reports neurological symptoms — dizziness, pressure headaches, hearing loss — he associates with a high-pitched buzzing sound in his apartment. This is widely considered the first documented Havana Syndrome case. |
| **January 2017** | State Department issues health alert about "incidents" affecting U.S. diplomats in Havana. |
| **February 2017** | A cluster of 16 cases confirmed among U.S. personnel in Havana. FBI deploys investigators. |
| **April 2017** | U.S. begins non-emergency departure of non-essential personnel from Cuba. |
| **September 2017** | State Department orders reduction of Cuban embassy staff in Washington. U.S. embassy in Havana shrinks to essential personnel only. |
| **August–September 2017** | CIA withdraws an entire station from Havana, an unprecedented move. |
| **November 2017** | State Department releases audio recordings of sounds heard by affected personnel. |
| **January 2018** | JASON Advisory Group concludes audio recordings are "most likely" Indies short-tailed crickets (*Anurogryllus celerinictus*). Canadian diplomats in Havana begin reporting similar symptoms. |
| **May 2018** | Guangzhou, China — U.S. consulate reports cluster of symptoms among Chinese staff, similar to those in Havana. CDC deploys investigators. |
| **November 2018** | University of Pennsylvania study published in *JAMA* identifies what it calls a "new syndrome" resembling persistent concussion; later criticized for methodological flaws and lack of control group. |

## 2019–2020: Scientific and Official Assessments

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **February 2019** | *Friedman study* of Canadian diplomats in published in *JAMA Otolaryngology* identifies vestibular and ocular motor abnormalities in Canadian victims. |
| **December 2019** | CDC publishes final report on Guangzhou cluster. |
| **November 2020** | State Department stops publishing AHI press briefings, drawing criticism from victims and transparency advocates. |
| **December 2020** | **NASEM (National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine)** final report concludes that "directed, pulsed radiofrequency (RF) energy" is the "most plausible" mechanism to explain the symptoms, though not conclusive. The report notes symptoms are "unlike any disorder in the neurological or general medical literature." |

## 2021–2022: Political Crisis and Intelligence Assessments

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **February 2021** | **ODNI (Office of the Director of National Intelligence)** establishes an interagency task force. Assessment underway across 7 intelligence agencies. |
| **July 2021** | CIA places Russian counterintelligence veteran in charge of Havana Syndrome investigation. Cases surge in Vienna — about two dozen officials report symptoms, making it the largest cluster outside Havana. |
| **October 2021** | **HAVANA Act of 2021** (Helping American Victims Afflicted by Neurological Attacks) unanimously passes the Senate and is signed into law by President Biden on October 8, 2021. Authorizes lump-sum compensation of up to $187,300 for qualifying brain injuries. |
| **2021–2022** | CIA Director William Burns warns Russian intelligence counterparts directly about the incidents and threatens "consequences." Publicly reported attacks appear to decline after these warnings — a possible deterrence effect. |
| **February 2022** | **CIA interim report** finds foreign adversary involvement "unlikely." Most of ~1,000 reported cases attributed to environmental factors, undiagnosed medical conditions, or stress. Foreign involvement not ruled out in roughly 24 cases. AHI cases continue to be reported in Vienna, Berlin, and other European posts. |
| **July 2022** | JASON Advisory Group second report (2022 review) rates RF energy as a "highly unlikely" cause, further deepening the scientific disagreement. |

## 2023–2024: The Narrative Shifts

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **February 2023** | **ODNI assessment** — Seven agencies conclude it is "very unlikely" a foreign adversary used a novel weapon or prototype device to harm U.S. government personnel. Five agencies join the majority view. Notably, symptoms are reported to be real and sincerely experienced. |
| **March 2023** | **Bartholomew & Baloh (PMC)** publish analysis suggesting mass psychogenic illness (MPI) as explanation, noting prevalence of misconceptions about MPI and the "nocebo effect." |
| **March 2024** | **Joint investigation by 60 Minutes, The Insider, and Der Spiegel** aired/released. Yearlong, extensively documented investigation uncovers evidence "suggesting that unexplained anomalous health incidents, also known as Havana Syndrome, may have their origin in the use of directed energy weapons wielded by members of Russian GRU Unit 29155." Links include geolocation data placing GRU officers at incident sites in Poland, Georgia, Australia, Taiwan, Vienna, and Hanoi. |
| **March 2024** | **NIH publishes two JAMA studies** of 86 patients finding no significant MRI-detectable brain injury or biological markers compared to a control group. Study criticized by victims for methodology and participant selection. |
| **August 2024** | **Global Affairs Canada report** — Canadian government concludes the unexplained health incidents "were not the result of a malicious act of a foreign actor." Victims outraged by the dismissal. |
| **September 2024** | **Senate Intelligence Committee report** finds CIA mishandling of AHI cases — significant disparity in claim approvals: only 21% of CIA applicants approved vs. 67% at other agencies. Identifies systemic issues in how CIA processed compensation applications. |
| **December 2024** | **Senate Intelligence Committee** releases summary of its investigation, finding evidence of CIA mishandling and institutional bias against victims. **House Intelligence Committee (HPSCI)** releases separate report finding foreign adversary involvement "increasingly likely," a direct contradiction to the ODNI/IC view. |

## 2025: Political Reversal

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **January 2025** | **ODNI update** — Two of seven intelligence agencies shift their assessment from "very unlikely" to "not unlikely" (roughly even chance) that a foreign adversary developed and deployed a novel weapon or prototype device against U.S. personnel. Rep. Mike Turner states a "foreign adversary is likely responsible," citing "direct evidence" that prior assessments lacked analytic integrity. |
| **April–June 2025** | **Congressional hearings** — House Homeland Security Subcommittee holds hearings on AHI. House Intelligence Committee Chairman Rick Crawford (R-AR) leads ongoing inquiry. Bipartisan congressional pressure for new investigation grows. Bipartisan senators urge Pentagon not to impact Havana Syndrome research and resources. |
| **July 2025** | Pentagon begins reviewing applications for HAVANA Act compensation for DoD personnel. GAO report confirms 334 Americans (including 15 children) qualify for treatment in specialized military health facilities. |
| **September–October 2025** | **Criminal referrals to DOJ** — House Intelligence Committee sends criminal referrals to the Department of Justice, identifying potentially illegal actions related to the IC's handling of AHI investigations and the dismissal/marginalization of victims. |
| **November 2024** | **White House Situation Room Meeting** — Senior NSC officials, Dr. Paul Friedrichs (White House Director of Office of Pandemic Preparedness), and AHI victims including Marc Polymeropoulos meet. Friedrichs apologizes, stating he had "never seen such neglect for victims in over three decades of practicing military medicine." |

## 2026: New Evidence and Restructuring

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **January 2026** | **Device Revelation** — CNN reports that DHS acquired a backpack-sized pulsed RF device in an undercover operation at the end of the Biden administration, allegedly costing millions of dollars and partially funded by the Pentagon. Device reportedly emits pulsed radio frequencies linked to Havana Syndrome. **January 14, 2026:** Chairman Andrew Garbarino (R-NY) and Committee members send letter to DHS Secretary Kristi Noem probing the device acquisition. |
| **February 2026** | **Norwegian scientist self-experimentation** — A Norwegian government scientist builds a microwave pulse device from classified information to test whether directed energy is harmful to humans. Tests the device on himself and experiences neurological symptoms similar to Havana Syndrome. CIA and Pentagon review the experiment. **February 24, 2026:** Pentagon announces restructuring of the AHI Cross-Functional Team (CFT), realigning it to a "technically-grounded organization" focused on "treatments and countermeasures." Senators Shaheen and Collins urge a pause until Congress is briefed. |
| **March 10, 2026** | **Marc Polymeropoulos** publishes "We Were Right About Havana Syndrome" in War on the Rocks. The 26-year CIA veteran details systemic "gaslighting" and medical neglect, argues that NSC officials privately conceded the IC approach had been "terribly flawed." Calls for declassification of the 2022 CIA Inspector General report, lifelong Walter Reed access, and a whole-of-government investigation. |
