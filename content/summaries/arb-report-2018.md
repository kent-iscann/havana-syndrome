## Overview

The ARB was convened to investigate mysterious health episodes at the U.S. Embassy in Havana. The report — heavily redacted — was a damning indictment of the State Department's response under Secretary Rex Tillerson, finding "**systemic disorganization,"** **lack of senior leadership,** and **failure to follow established procedures.**

## Key Findings

### Leadership Failures
- **No designated official** with overall responsibility for the crisis — the "single most significant deficiency"
- The decision to reduce embassy staff by 60% in September 2017 was made **without a formal risk/benefit analysis**
- The ARB was only convened in February 2018, months after the crisis escalated, and only after **political pressure from Senator Marco Rubio**

### CIA Involvement
- Confirmed the CIA **closed its Havana station** and withdrew all personnel in September 2017
- Two CIA "temporary duty" (TDY) officers fell ill at the [[hotel-nacional-havana]] in August 2017
- The ARB criticized the CIA for **"excessive secrecy"** that prevented the State Department from reacting quickly

### Global Spread
- Confirmed case at [[guangzhou-consulate]] — an employee suffered injuries matching Havana victims
- Similar incidents reported in **Tashkent** and one other **redacted country**
- Reports from other countries received by the board (details redacted)

### Obstruction of CDC Research
- State Department commissioned the CDC to investigate in late 2017
- State lawyers then **blocked the study for nearly a year** citing privacy restrictions
- Impasse resolved only in November 2018 after the ARB intervened

## Critical Excerpts

> "The mechanism for the cause of the injuries is currently unknown. We do not know the motive behind these incidents, when they actually commenced, or who did it... We do know that USG, and Canadian diplomatic community members, were injured, but we do not know how."

> "The Department of State's response to these incidents was characterized by a lack of senior leadership, ineffective communications, and systemic disorganization."
