## Overview

This is the **most authoritative scientific assessment** of Anomalous Health Incidents to date. A 19-member multidisciplinary committee was commissioned by the Department of State to evaluate the clinical features, plausible mechanisms, and response protocols for the unexplained health incidents in Havana (2016) and Guangzhou (2017).

The report concluded that **directed, pulsed radiofrequency (RF) energy** was the **"most plausible mechanism"** for explaining the acute symptoms — a finding that has been cited extensively by the directed energy proponent camp ever since.

## Key Finding — The Distinctive Clinical Constellation

> "The committee finds this constellation of acute symptoms with directional and location-specific features to be very unusual, and to the best of its knowledge, **unlike any disorder reported in the neurological or general medical literature**." — **NASEM Committee**

## Clinical Features

### Biphasic Illness Pattern

#### Acute Phase (Most Distinctive)
- Sudden onset of loud sounds (screeching, chirping, clicking, piercing)
- Intense sensation of head pressure or vibration
- Pain in one or both ears or across broad region of head
- Symptoms perceived as originating from a **specific direction** or restricted to a **specific physical location**
- **Immediate relief upon moving to a different room** — suggests physical force that could penetrate windows but not walls

#### Chronic Phase (Less Specific)
- Vestibular: Dizziness, impaired balance, unsteady gait
- Cognitive/Psychological: Impaired concentration, memory deficits, fatigue, depression, insomnia
- Physical: Chronic headaches, tinnitus

### Neurological Localization

> "This combination of distinctive, acute, auditory-vestibular symptoms suggests an effect localized to the **labyrinth** or **VIII cranial nerve** or its **brainstem connections**." — **NASEM Committee**

### Canadian Cohort Difference

The Canadian Embassy personnel in Havana reported chronic symptoms (vertigo, tinnitus) but **lacked the acute directional/auditory phase** that U.S. personnel experienced — suggesting different or additional causes for the Canadian cases.

## Evaluation of Plausible Mechanisms

### 1. Directed RF Energy — **MOST PLAUSIBLE**

| Evidence | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Frey Effect** | Pulsed RF energy (0.4-3 GHz) induces audible sounds (clicks, buzzes) inside the head without external noise |
| **Consistency** | Acute symptoms (clicking, pressure, directional sounds, hearing loss, tinnitus, impaired balance) align with known RF effects |
| **Non-thermal effects** | Symptoms occurred without heating or electronic device damage — suggesting cell membrane dysfunction or voltage-gated calcium channel disruption |
| **Chronic effects** | RF exposure consistent with headache, fatigue, dizziness, cognitive deficits |
| **Russian/Soviet research** | Documented that **pulsing** is most critical factor in biological effects |

### 2. Chemical Exposures (Pesticides) — **UNLIKELY**

- Organophosphate (OP) and pyrethroid insecticides used in Zika fumigation in Cuba
- **No evidence** of acute high-level organophosphate poisoning (cholinergic crisis)
- Canadian study suggesting OP toxicity was deemed flawed (measured AChE in serum, not whole blood)
- Caveat: Could not rule out that pesticide exposure increased susceptibility or contributed to chronic symptoms

### 3. Infectious Agents (Zika) — **HIGHLY UNLIKELY**

- Zika peaked in Cuba 2016-2017
- Neurological complications from Zika occur in only 0.1-0.2% of cases
- Zika presents with fever, rash, joint pain — **absent** in DOS cohort
- Sudden onset and specific constellation do not match Zika or tropical diseases

### 4. Psychological/Social Factors — **CONTRIBUTING**

- **Cannot explain** the initial sudden-onset audio-vestibular signs
- Mass psychogenic illness requires contact-tracing evidence committee did not have
- ~25% of NIH patients diagnosed with **Persistent Postural-Perceptual Dizziness (PPPD)** — a **functional** (not psychiatric) disorder triggered by vestibular insult

## Diagnostic Testing Assessment

### MRI Imaging
- **Conventional structural MRIs** were essentially normal
- Advanced studies (Verma et al., 2019) suggested group differences in brain volume/connectivity but lacked replication cohort — insufficient for individual diagnosis

### Vestibular/Balance Testing
- High rates of impairment found
- Tests (dynamic posturography) cannot distinguish between structural, functional, or psychiatric causes

### Neuropsychological Testing
- No distinct pattern of clinically diagnosable cognitive deficits
- Often showed "psychological distress" typical of chronic illness

## Recommendations

### 1. Baseline Data Collection
- Collect baseline blood, plasma, urine, and neurological/visual/auditory exams for **all** personnel before overseas assignments
- Establish ongoing registries for affected cohorts

### 2. Rapid Response Protocols
- Pre-authorized plans for comprehensive public health investigations
- Activate **immediately** upon discovery of new case cluster
- CDC involvement should not be delayed (it was 1 year for Cuba)

### 3. Real-Time Environmental Monitoring
- If RF energy is suspected, **equip personnel with RF measurement tools**
- Deploy stationary or wearable sensors for chemical/biological/physical agents

### 4. Treatment Best Practices
- Expert evaluation **as early as possible** after symptom onset
- Interdisciplinary rehabilitation (cognitive, neuro-optometric, vestibular)
- 10-20% of PPPD patients remain work-disabled after 4+ years despite maximal treatment

## Investigation Challenges

- 19-member committee (largest ever assembled for this topic)
- Lacked access to individual-level health records due to HIPAA
- Could not link data across different clinical providers
- China data deemed "too sparse and fragmentary" for substantive conclusions
- Different agencies had access to different data sets due to security clearances
