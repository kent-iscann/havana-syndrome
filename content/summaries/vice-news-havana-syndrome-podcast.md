Award-winning journalists Jon Lee Anderson (The New Yorker) and Adam Entous (The New Yorker) produced this 8-episode investigative podcast for VICE World News. The series draws on their extensive reporting including their landmark New Yorker article "The Mystery of the Havana Syndrome."

## Overview

A mysterious, debilitating illness begins afflicting American diplomats and spies working abroad starting in late 2016 in Havana, Cuba, then spreading to cities worldwide. Victims report crippling neurological symptoms described as the sensation of being hit by an invisible, directed pressure. The podcast pursues three core questions: What is it? Who is causing it? Why has the U.S. government struggled to provide a definitive answer?

## Episode Guide

### 1. Get Off The X
Jon Lee Anderson and Adam Entous set out to solve what happened to Tina Onufer and other early victims. Introduces the concept of "getting off the X" -- moving away from the source of the sound/pressure caused the sensation to stop instantly. Features the first documented case of a staffer at the U.S. embassy in Havana who experienced a bizarre incident in her kitchen.

### 2. The Diplomat and The Spy
Explores the friction between Obama administration officials attempting to restore diplomatic trust with Cuba and the CIA's conflicting clandestine operations that exploited the rapprochement. Features former CIA Director [[john-brennan]] discussing the intelligence opportunities created by the 2014 thaw in U.S.-Cuba relations.

### 3. Immaculate Concussion
Details the secret transport of patients to the University of Pennsylvania where doctors discovered neurological damage comparable to concussions despite no physical head trauma. The Penn study concluded the ailments were real and not "mass psychogenic illness" (hysteria).

### 4. Adios y Hasta Nunca
The hosts travel to Havana to investigate the earliest Havana Syndrome cases and visit the original "crime scenes." What they find on the ground fuels more questions than answers.

### 5. Anyone, Anywhere, At Any Time
The U.S. consulate in Guangzhou, China becomes the site of a mysterious outbreak of illnesses, and the White House is forced to reassess. The phenomenon spreads from a localized Cuban issue to a global threat.

### 6. If There's a 'There' There
Adam receives an anonymous tip and investigates decades-old incidents in which the Soviet Union repeatedly aimed microwaves at the U.S. Embassy in Moscow (the "Moscow Signal"). Explores the Cold War precedent for directed energy targeting.

### 7. Vienna Rules
The largest outbreak of Havana Syndrome illnesses is reported in Vienna, Austria. Adam and Jon Lee travel there to explore why the city -- a known hub for international espionage -- has become the epicenter. The Vienna outbreak led to the dismissal of the local CIA station chief. The hosts also investigate a report of symptoms at a hotel near Buckingham Palace in London and an incident within shouting distance of the Oval Office.

### 8. Deny Everything, Admit Nothing
Season finale. Adam and Jon Lee try to piece everything together and find a major hole in their findings. A mysterious source comes forward to fill in the blanks.

## Key Findings and Angles

- **Cuban Harassment Escalation:** Before the attacks, CIA officers reported aggressive harassment by Cuban intelligence including home break-ins, cutting internet lines, draining water cisterns, and killing pets. In 2016, the CIA's Western Hemisphere Mission Center "took the gloves off," and harassment escalated further.
- **The Moscow Connection:** Draws parallels between modern Havana Syndrome cases and the Cold War-era "Moscow Signal" where the Soviet Union beamed microwaves at the U.S. Embassy in Moscow for years.
- **Geopolitical Impact:** The syndrome effectively derailed the Obama-era rapprochement with Cuba. Cuban hardliners may have acted to halt normalization of relations.
- **Global Spread Pattern:** Outbreaks peaked during U.S. presidential transitions (2016 and 2020) in "diplomatic battlefields" including Havana, Vienna, London, Moscow, and Washington D.C.
- **Medical Gaslighting:** The podcast documents internal CIA resistance -- some officials told victims to "man up" and dismissed claims as stress. A former CIA physician who investigated the cases himself came down with the same symptoms. "Dr. Andrew" alleged the CIA sought outside experts specifically to confirm the syndrome wasn't real.
- **The "Tony" Case:** An undercover CIA officer (pseudonym) described a "loud sound" blasting into his bedroom in December 2016, followed by severe ear pain, gushing nosebleeds, cognitive decline, and inability to perform basic tasks. "Within six months, I was a zombie and nonfunctional as a human being."
