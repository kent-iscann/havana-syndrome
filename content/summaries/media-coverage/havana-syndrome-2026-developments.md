## Overview

2026 has emerged as a watershed year for Havana Syndrome (Anomalous Health Incidents, AHIs). Multiple converging developments — including the Pentagon's confirmation of a physical device, a Norwegian scientist's self-experimentation, sweeping policy changes under the Trump administration, and a bitter internal intelligence community dispute — have fundamentally reshaped the debate. The narrative has shifted from "likely nothing happening" to "a foreign adversary is likely responsible."

## Device Recovery and Pentagon Testing

### HSI/DoD Undercover Acquisition

In January 2026, CNN and other outlets revealed that **Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)** had purchased a device allegedly linked to Havana Syndrome in an undercover operation during the final weeks of the Biden administration (late 2024). Key details:

- **Cost:** An "eight-figure" sum — tens of millions of dollars — reportedly funded at least partially by the **Department of War (DoD)**
- **Mechanism:** After more than a year of study, Pentagon investigators determined the device emits **pulses of radio waves**
- **Portability:** Compact enough to fit inside a **backpack**
- **Origin:** The device contains **Russian components**, though it is not entirely Russian-made
- **Recovery Network:** According to **[[60-minutes-investigation-2024|60 Minutes]]** reporting, the device was obtained through a sophisticated **Russian criminal network**, allegedly involving Venezuelan connections
- **Briefings:** Defense officials briefed the House and Senate Intelligence Committees on the device and its testing results in late 2025

The revelation has been cited by victims as vindication. Former CIA officer **Marc Polymeropoulos** stated: "If the [US government] has indeed uncovered such devices, then the CIA owes all the victims a f\*\*king major and public apology for how we have been treated as pariahs."

**Note:** See [[venezuela-device-recovery-2026|Venezuela Device Recovery 2026]] for related coverage of the device recovery's connection to criminal networks involving Venezuelan and Russian actors.

## Congressional Oversight Actions

### House Committee on Homeland Security Investigation

On **January 15, 2026**, House Committee on Homeland Security Chairman **Andrew Garbarino (R-NY)** led six subcommittee chairmen in sending a formal letter to DHS Secretary **Kristi Noem**, probing the device acquisition. The letter requested:
1. The procurement process and timeline
2. The exact cost of the device
3. All testing results
4. The operational purpose for acquiring the device

The letter was co-signed by Subcommittee Chairmen Michael Guest, Carlos Gimenez, August Pfluger, Josh Brecheen, Andy Ogles, and Dale Strong.

**Context:** The investigation followed a May 2024 hearing featuring testimony from [[christo-grozev|Christo Grozev]], [[greg-edgreen|Lt. Col. Greg Edgreen]], and [[mark-zaid|Mark Zaid]] on the subject of AHIs.

### Trump Administration and Intelligence Community Reassessment

Under the Trump administration, Havana Syndrome has been elevated back to a priority issue:

- **DNI Tulsi Gabbard** has promised a "fresh look" at the issue and a potential new Intelligence Community Assessment (ICA)
- **CIA Director John Ratcliffe** has committed to reopening investigations and recalling previous "flawed" analysis
- In **October 2025**, the House Intelligence Committee sent criminal referrals to the DOJ regarding potential illegal actions by intelligence officials during the initial 2023 ODNI investigation
- The **Intelligence Community Assessment** process was restarted, with ODNI reviewing the new physical evidence from the recovered device

A reported February 2026 dispute between ODNI and CIA emerged, with the NY Post reporting that **Gabbard and CIA were "at war" over bombshell evidence linking Havana Syndrome to Russia.**

## Norwegian Scientist Self-Experimentation

In **February 2026**, *The Washington Post* reported an extraordinary story from Norway:

A **Norwegian government scientist**, skeptical that pulsed-energy weapons could cause neurological damage, built a microwave pulse-emitting device in **2024** and tested it on himself in an attempt to prove such devices were harmless.

**Result:** He suffered neurological symptoms similar to those reported by Havana Syndrome victims. The experiment was conducted in strict secrecy.

The U.S. intelligence community (CIA and Pentagon) subsequently reviewed the Norwegian experiment. While the symptoms were described as not an exact match for "classic" Havana Syndrome, the incident provides rare physical evidence that pulsed electromagnetic devices can indeed affect human biology — a point that advocates of the directed-energy hypothesis have seized upon.

**Key significance:** The Norwegian experiment challenges the intelligence community's longstanding claim that the technology to cause directed-energy harm at Havana Syndrome levels simply does not exist.

## Pentagon Task Force Restructuring (February 2026)

On **February 24, 2026**, the Pentagon officially moved the **Anomalous Health Incidents (AHI) Cross-Functional Team (CFT)** from the policy directorate to the **Undersecretariat for Research and Engineering (R&E)**. Key changes:

- **New Oversight:** Under Peter Highnam, Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Critical Technologies
- **Military Leadership:** **Rear Adm. Michael J. Thornton** (triple-board-certified physician, former Navy SEAL) appointed as military assistant to the CFT
- **Mission Shift:** From policy coordination to "treatments and countermeasures" — developing technical "antidotes" to neutralize adversarial technologies
- **Goal:** "Restores rigorous discipline and accelerates the integration of research into tangible medical treatments and countermeasures"

The move drew bipartisan concern. Senators **Jeanne Shaheen** and **Susan Collins** urged the Pentagon to pause the relocation until Congress could be consulted, fearing it might weaken the team's standing.

## Marc Polymeropoulos: "We Were Right" (March 2026)

On **March 10, 2026**, former CIA officer **Marc Polymeropoulos** published a comprehensive piece in *War on the Rocks* titled "We Were Right About Havana Syndrome," documenting:

- His own 2017 AHI experience and WRI treatment
- The "gaslighting" and denigration of victims by CIA senior leadership
- The **November 12, 2024 Situation Room Meeting** where NSC officials told victims: "We believe you" and "We failed you"
- White House Dr. Paul Friedrichs' admission of unprecedented medical neglect
- Three pillars for resolution: accountability (declassify 2022 IG report), healthcare access (lifelong WRI coverage), and attribution/response (whole-of-government investigation)

The article became a rallying point for victim advocates and helped shape the Trump administration's renewed approach.

## Canada Response (March 2026)

**March 20, 2026:** Global News Canada reported that Ottawa stands by its own Havana Syndrome report even as the U.S. reconsiders its position on foreign involvement. The Canadian government had previously concluded that directed energy was the most likely cause of symptoms.

## Cuba Policy Context

**March 2026:** As the Trump administration increased pressure on Cuba through renewed sanctions and regime change rhetoric, media attention on Havana Syndrome resurfaced. The intersection of Cuba policy and Havana Syndrome investigations created renewed focus on the original 2016 incidents at the U.S. Embassy in Havana.

## Key Debates Still Unresolved (April 2026)

1. **Device Attribution:** Whether the recovered device is definitively linked to reported AHI cases or is an unrelated prototype
2. **State Actor Attribution:** Whether Russia, or another nation-state, conducted the original attacks
3. **Scope:** Whether the original 2016 Cuba cases were targeted anti-personnel operations or collateral damage from espionage equipment
4. **Criminal Accountability:** Whether DOJ will pursue charges based on the October 2025 criminal referrals
5. **New ICA:** Whether ODNI's reassessment will reach different conclusions than the 2023 assessment
