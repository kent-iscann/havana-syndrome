From the team behind *Fat Leonard*, *Resistance: Stories from Ukraine*, and *Kabul Falling*, THE SOUND is a production of [[project-brazen]] and [[goat-rodeo]] in partnership with [[prx]]. Hosted by Nicky Woolf, the podcast is accompanied by a docuseries (available on YouTube).

## Overview

In December 2016, a U.S. official in Havana went to the embassy medical center to report a debilitating and confounding illness. Symptoms included headaches, nausea, hearing loss, memory and vision problems -- and an onset characterized by hearing a buzzing, hissing, grinding sound. Soon a second, third, and fourth official came down with the same symptoms. By summer 2017, dozens of U.S. diplomats and some Canadians had what was becoming known as "Havana Syndrome."

THE SOUND explores spycraft, technology, and the brain in a story where "at every turn, you're never quite sure what's real and what's an illusion — a story about how the Cold War, maybe, never really ended."

## Episode Guide

### Trailer
Sets up the central mystery: a mysterious outbreak of neurological problems among American spies and diplomats in Cuba.

### Chapter 1: Jericho
The initial outbreak in Havana. Features never-before-heard accounts from the first victims and an analysis of classified intelligence to determine the starting point of the crisis.
- **Guests:** Kevin Coats, Patrick Oppman

### Chapter 2: The Immaculate Concussion
Cases are spreading and investigators flock to Cuba while politicians start hunting for a culprit. Examines early investigations by both U.S. and Cuban governments and the formation of a political narrative around the "attacks."
- **Guests:** Doug Ferguson, James Giordano, Johana Tablada, Mitchell Valdez-Sosa

### Chapter 3: Speak of the Devil
Skepticism and the "psychogenic" theory. A critical look at whether the symptoms are physical or psychological. Woolf enlists a mentalist to explore the possibility of mass psychogenic illness.
- **Guests:** Robert Bartholomew, Bob Baloh, Matt Cooper

### Chapter 4: The Frey Effect
The science of microwave energy. As cases appear globally, investigators look into the "Frey Effect" -- the human perception of audible clicks or even speech induced by pulsed or modulated radio frequencies.
- **Guests:** Beatrice Golomb, Linda Birnbaum, David Relman, Frank Figliuzzi, Charles Kupperman

### Chapter 5: The Great British Nuke Off
Weaponization and the Military-Industrial Complex. The team travels to the UK to attempt to build a microwave device capable of causing these symptoms, exploring the future of directed-energy warfare.
- **Guests:** Kyle Major, James Giordano

### Chapter 6: A Silent Injury
The Moscow connection and historical context. A CIA officer falls ill in Moscow, shifting the narrative toward Russia. Explores decades of Soviet radiation research and the "never-ending" Cold War.
- **Guests:** Marc Polymeropoulos, Aliza Bran, James Schumacher, Peter Kornbluh

### Chapter 7: The Two Exits
On-the-ground reporting in Cuba. A visit to the original "crime scenes" in Havana to find the source of "the sound" amidst the reopening of the U.S. Embassy and mass Cuban migration.
- **Guests:** Johana Tablada, Raul Rodriguez, Patrick Oppman

### Chapter 8: Hammers and Nails
The finale. Woolf attempts to answer the central question: What happened? Charts the future for victims and the ongoing search for accountability.
- **Guests:** Marc Polymeropoulos, Ken Jones, Doug Ferguson, Kevin Coats

### Bonus: Live from the International Spy Museum
A panel discussion on global intelligence and espionage recorded live in Washington D.C., recorded in collaboration with *SpyCast*.
- **Guests:** Dr. Andrew Hammond (moderator), Marc Polymeropoulos, Mark Zaid

## Key Themes and Angles

- **Agency Disagreement:** The podcast documents how U.S. agencies failed to agree even on basic premises. The State Department initially said it was a "sonic device," then rolled that back. The CIA hinted at microwave radiation. The FBI said there was no "there there" -- then seemed to roll that back as well.
- **The Theory Spectrum:** Woolf explores the full range of theories -- from noisy crickets and mosquito spray to mass delusion to targeted directed-energy weapons -- weighing evidence and speaking to skeptics and believers alike.
- **Hands-On Investigation:** Unusually for a podcast, the team travels to the UK to attempt building a microwave device, testing whether such technology could plausibly cause the reported symptoms.
- **Cold War Continuity:** The series frames Havana Syndrome through the lens of an unfinished Cold War, drawing connections to Soviet microwave research and decades of intelligence competition.
- **Victim-Centered:** The podcast dedicates significant space to hearing from the victims themselves, exploring the medical, professional, and psychological toll of the illness.

## Distinguishing Features

Unlike the [[vice-news-havana-syndrome-podcast|VICE News Havana Syndrome Podcast]], which focuses heavily on the geopolitical and intelligence-community angle, *The Sound* takes a broader approach -- dedicating chapters to the psychogenic theory, the physics of microwave energy, a practical attempt to build a device, and the human cost. Its original score by the Attacca Quartet gives it a distinctive audio aesthetic, and the accompanying YouTube docuseries provides visual supplements to the audio investigation.
