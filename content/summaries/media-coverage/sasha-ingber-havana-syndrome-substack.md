## Piece 1: Exclusive — U.S. Has Been Testing a Device Linked to Havana Syndrome (January 12, 2026)

### The Scoop
Ingber broke news that the U.S. government possesses and is testing at least one device linked to AHIs. Two sources confirmed the device was seized by U.S. Special Forces during an operation, while other reporting suggested it was purchased on the black market. The device is reportedly held under the Department of Defense's Intelligence & Security unit.

### Device Capabilities
- Mechanism: Directed energy — pulsed radio frequencies
- Can produce silent emissions or loud audible sounds
- Causes incapacitation, vomiting, loss of bowel control, internal injuries without being lethal
- Historical precedent: In 2018, Israel reportedly asked the Pentagon if this type of weapon could be used for crowd control against Palestinians; Secretary of Defense James Mattis declined

### The Maduro Raid Connection
The investigation was partially sparked by claims from a Venezuelan security guard following a Delta Force raid on Nicolas Maduro. The guard alleged U.S. use of a sound-wave weapon to incapacitate personnel, describing immediate nosebleeds and vomiting. Ingber notes skepticism from intel sources — these acute symptoms differ from typical Havana Syndrome manifestation — but acknowledges the claim brought the device question to the surface.

### Internal IC Conflict
A significant divide over transparency:
- **Pro-release**: DNI Tulsi Gabbard, NCTC Director Joseph Kent
- **Anti-release**: CIA Director John Ratcliffe, DOD Under Secretary Bradley Hansell
- A new ODNI report ("the dusty report") was finished but unreleased for months
- Gabbard's spokesperson: dismissing Americans affected by AHIs is "unacceptable"

### Key Quote
> "Two people told me that a number of months ago, the U.S. captured a weapon that has been associated with Havana Syndrome. Both said it was seized by U.S. Special Forces during an operation."

— Legal confirmation from Mark Zaid: "I can confirm that I had the same information of USG possessing one or more such devices — and from different sources."

### Venezuelan Guard Account
> "It was like a very intense sound wave... Suddenly I felt like my head was exploding from the inside. We all started bleeding from the nose. Some were vomiting blood."

## Piece 2: A Rift in the Intelligence Community — Officers Reporting Havana Syndrome See Little Compensation (May 8, 2024)

### The Compensation Crisis
- Only ~5% of affected personnel have received compensation under the HAVANA Act
- Implementation is inconsistent across agencies
  - CIA and State Department: compensating piecemeal
  - FBI: only proposed regulations in April 2024
  - DOD: no process established at time of reporting
  - VA: no specific diagnostic code for AHI
- Defense Health Agency facing expiration of designated funds

### Evidence of Russian Involvement
Greg Edgreen (retired Army Lt. Col., former lead of the DIA investigation) outlined key indicators:
- Vladimir Putin's 2023 public references to "new physical principles weapons" including "radio-frequency arms"
- Nikolai Patrushev's (Russian Security Council Secretary) claim that hundreds of foreign intelligence agents were "neutralized" — Edgreen: "If Patrushev did not mean directed energy attacks, then the question is, well, where else are we losing hundreds of intelligence officers, guys?"

### Why the U.S. May Be Downplaying
National security attorney Mark Zaid outlined four theories:
1. **Act of war** — Attributing attacks to Russia on U.S. soil demands a costly response
2. **Operational security** — Admitting inability to protect personnel would devastate morale
3. **Reciprocal technology** — U.S. may possess or be developing similar capabilities
4. **Financial liability** — Long-term costs for hundreds of victims would be astronomical

### The Human Toll
- Victims experience altered speech patterns, brain fog, light sensitivity
- Edgreen warns of future health issues: "rare cancers, heart diseases, and tumors"
- Mark Zaid on the invisibility of injuries: "I wish I had been shot... so I can just pull my shirt up and show you the bullet hole"
- Some officers resigned rather than accept Russia-sphere assignments
> "I think it's time to fight back in the shadows... if we get hit in the face, we need to hit back in the face twice as hard." — Greg Edgreen

## Piece 3: At a Havana Syndrome Conference in Philly (Date: ~early 2026)

### Event: FPRI Conference
Ingber reported from the Foreign Policy Research Institute conference in Philadelphia, organized by Philip Wasielewski (Director of Center for Intelligence and Nontraditional Warfare).

### Expert Testimony — Dr. David Relman (Stanford)
Relman, who led the National Academies of Science 2020 report and worked with the IC, warned:
> "Regardless of what happened to this large number of individuals, in the future, if we don't understand this better, we are simply going to become the unwitting victims of others who now see that there's an opportunity to cause harm in a way that causes great dissension and confusion."

Key points from Relman:
- The IC study examined devices but was specifically "asked not to look at potential adversaries"
- There was a "deliberate effort to try to tamp this down" as cases increased
- His team recommended "devices to aid research" to the IC

### New Victim Disclosures
**Ret. Lt. Col. Chris Schlagheck (U.S. Space Force)** — first public appearance:
- Attacked five times at his home in Northern Virginia
- A Russian family moved in across the street and vanished shortly after incidents
- Diagnosed with 40 conditions including TBI, PTSD, and sensorial/neural hearing loss

### Counterintelligence Nightmare
- One victim declined to attend, calling the conference a "counterintelligence nightmare"
- The same victim claimed Russian spies tracked him in Philadelphia while he received medical treatment at the University of Pennsylvania
- Reports of incompetence, delays, and persistent government skepticism
- No will to test children of affected families
