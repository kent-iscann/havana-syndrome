## Overview

Dr. Antony Stokes, a 30-year UK Foreign and Commonwealth Office diplomat who served as British Ambassador to Cuba from late 2016 through 2022, is publishing an insider account of the Anomalous Health Incidents (AHI) phenomenon as it unfolded on the ground in Havana. His Substack *Ambassador at Large* features a multi-part piece titled "Inside Havana Syndrome" as well as related posts across his platforms. Stokes's perspective is that of a senior career diplomat who lived through the crisis firsthand, initially constrained by government protocol while in service, now free to speak independently. His tone is measured, diplomatic, but increasingly direct about the credibility of directed energy weapon theories.

## Timeline on the Ground in Havana

### Arrival and First Contact (Late 2016)
Stokes arrived in Havana shortly after the death of Fidel Castro. Within weeks, he was summoned by his U.S. counterpart at the U.S. Embassy for a "high-security confidential briefing" about mysterious incidents affecting American diplomatic personnel.

### The Mystery Deepens (2017)
The phenomenon spread to affect British and Canadian diplomatic staff as well. Stokes notes that the situation "got worse throughout my posting." Key symptoms reported by affected staff:
- Painful, localized auditory sensations ("painful sounds")
- Nausea and dizziness
- Persistent forgetfulness and cognitive impairment
- Sleep disturbances

He emphasizes a core leadership dilemma: "Rule 1 for Ambassadors: 'staff safety comes first'. But how to protect colleagues and families, when nobody then understood what from?"

### Embassy Evacuations (2017–2018)
The U.S. eventually evacuated its embassy to a skeleton staff. The Canadian government also withdrew most of its diplomatic presence. Stokes remained at post and had to balance diplomatic duties with personnel safety amid an unknown and poorly understood threat.

### UK Government Cable Leak (2019)
*The Guardian* reported on UK diplomatic cables that shed light on the "sonic attacks" scare. One cable, copied to Anthony Stokes, noted that the press "broadly buy the veracity" of a Cuban government statement denying responsibility. This suggests Stokes was in direct communication about UK assessments from London.

## Stokes's Key Arguments and Perspectives

### On Directed Energy Weapons as the Likely Culprit
Stokes has taken a public position that directed energy weapons are the "biggest threat to diplomats and humanity" and that the 2016 Cuba attack was a landmark event in their deployment. On LinkedIn he wrote: "The biggest threat to diplomats and humanity is that of directed energy weapons. The attack in 2016 in Cuba (Havana Syndrome) is now commonplace among civilians."

### On the Device
He references CNN reports that the U.S. Department of Defense has been testing a device believed to be related to the Havana Syndrome attacks. Technical characteristics, including to unnamed sources:
- Small enough to fit into a backpack
- Produces pulsed radio waves
- Acquired through an undercover operation

### On the Broader Geopolitical Context
Stokes places Havana Syndrome within a wider frame of diplomatic and security challenges:
- A pivotal year for diplomacy in 2026
- Compounding events in Venezuela
- The deteriorating situation for Cuban citizens
- The intersection of AHI investigations with shifting U.S. intelligence assessments

### Historical Precedent
Commenters on his posts (which Stokes engages with) reference the Moscow Signal — Cold War-era microwave irradiation of the U.S. Embassy in Moscow — as a direct historical parallel. Stokes appears to view Havana Syndrome as part of this longer lineage of energy-based aggression against diplomatic missions.

## Assessment: Perspective and Angle

**Supportive/Investigative.** Stokes is not a medical or technical expert but provides a unique diplomatic eyewitness perspective. He has consistently treated the phenomenon as real and serious, framing it as a security and diplomatic challenge. He leans toward the directed energy weapon hypothesis and has publicly stated that the Cuba incident was a watershed moment for directed energy threats. His insider access and diplomatic credentials lend weight to his accounts, though he is not making technical claims himself.
