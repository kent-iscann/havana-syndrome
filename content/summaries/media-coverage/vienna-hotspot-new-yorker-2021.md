## Overview

A follow-up reporting from *The New Yorker* (May 2021) documenting the emergence of Vienna as the second-largest hub for Havana Syndrome cases after Havana itself.

**Source:** The New Yorker  
**Date:** May 2021  
**Type:** News feature / Analysis  
**Ingested:** 2026-04-13

## Key Findings

### Vienna as the New Epicenter
- Approximately **two dozen** U.S. intelligence officers, diplomats, and government officials have reported symptoms in Vienna since early 2021
- Vienna now has more reported cases than any other city except Havana itself
- The Vienna cluster emerged under the Biden Administration, suggesting the phenomenon was ongoing and not limited to the Havana timeframe

### CIA Director Burns' Stance
- CIA Director William Burns privately referred to the incidents as **"attacks"**
- This language represented a significant shift from the more cautious terminology used in official communications ("anomalous health incidents")

### Medical Findings
University of Pennsylvania specialists described the brain damage as a **"concussion without a concussion"** — signs of physical damage despite no impact to the skull.

### The GRU Theory
U.S. officials in both the Trump and Biden administrations suspect the **GRU (Russian military intelligence)** is responsible:
- **Method:** Microwave-radiation devices aimed at U.S. personnel
- **Possible motive:** Stealing data from electronics or forcing U.S. diplomatic retreats (as in Havana)
- **Lack of proof:** Despite strong suspicion, no hard evidence has been made public
