## Overview

BBC documentary series investigating Havana Syndrome, presented by BBC Security Correspondent **Gordon Corera**. The series covers the origin of the mysterious illness affecting American diplomats and spies, beginning with the strange sounds reported in Cuba in 2016.

**Source:** BBC Audio / Crossing Continents / The Documentary Podcast  
**Author/Presenter:** Gordon Corera  
**Date:** 2021  
**Type:** Radio documentary / Audio documentary  
**Ingested:** 2026-04-13

## Key Findings

### What's Covered
- The origin story of Havana Syndrome from the first reports in Cuba (2016)
- Strange sounds heard by affected officials
- The spread of incidents beyond Cuba to other diplomatic posts
- Various scientific and intelligence theories about the cause
- Interviews with experts, officials, and those affected

### BBC's Additional Coverage
The BBC has covered Havana Syndrome multiple times through the same correspondent:
- **Crossing Continents** — In-depth examination of the global spread
- **What in the World** — Analysis segment: "Havana syndrome: What's causing it?" with Gordon Corera walking through theories

### Significance
Gordon Corera's reporting represents major international public broadcaster coverage of the phenomenon, lending credibility and global reach to the story beyond U.S.-centric media. The BBC treatment is notable for its balanced approach, examining multiple hypotheses without endorsing any single theory.
