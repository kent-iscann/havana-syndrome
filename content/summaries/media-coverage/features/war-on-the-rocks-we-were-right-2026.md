## Overview

Marc Polymeropoulos, a decorated 26-year CIA veteran, published this firsthand account on War on the Rocks in March 2026. He was personally affected by Havana Syndrome while serving as the CIA's Chief of Mission in Russia. The piece argues that the U.S. intelligence community — specifically the CIA — engaged in a "massive coverup" and actively "gaslit" victims for years before recent government stance shifts began to validate victims' claims that foreign adversary attacks are responsible. Polymeropoulos's unique perspective combines victim testimony, insider knowledge of CIA culture, and policy recommendations for the Trump administration.

## Personal Impact

Polymeropoulos was himself a victim of AHI. The physical and psychological toll forced him out of government service:

> "I was never the same after the incident and years of painful recovery continue to this day... my departure from government was not voluntary, it was by necessity."

He provides a cost figure that underscores his point about healthcare access: a month of intensive traumatic brain injury (TBI) treatment at Walter Reed National Military Medical Center cost him only $37 in administrative fees — yet was life-saving. His experience contrasts sharply with the systemic healthcare barriers most victims face.

## The Institutional Gaslighting Allegation

Polymeropoulos provides the most direct insider allegation yet about CIA culture toward victims:

> "At the CIA, senior leaders... often privately denigrated victims — including me — by accusing us of fabricating our conditions for financial gain."

He frames this as a "moral injury" that was as damaging as the physical injuries themselves — the betrayal by one's own institution after being harmed by an adversary.

Reports surfaced that the U.S. government purchased a device on the black market that:
- Produces pulsed radio waves

### Norwegian Investigation
The Norwegian government reportedly investigated and tested similar devices, directly contradicting earlier U.S. intelligence community claims that the technology did not exist.

### Congressional Shift
- January 2025: Rep. Mike Turner (Chair, House Intelligence Committee) stated that "a foreign adversary is likely responsible"
- A House Intelligence subcommittee report alleged the 2023 ODNI assessment was "developed in a manner inconsistent with analytic integrity"

## The Turning Point: November 12, 2024 White House Situation Room Meeting

A pivotal meeting between affected officers and the National Security Council (NSC):

### What Officials Said
- "We believe you"
- "We failed you"

### Medical Condemnation
Dr. Paul Friedrichs (Director of the Office of Pandemic Preparedness) apologized to victims, stating he had "never seen such neglect in 30 years of military medicine."

### Internal Conflict Exposed
- NSC emerged as an ally to victims
- CIA was viewed as "recalcitrant" and obstructive
- Polymeropoulos frames this as a fundamental institutional failure within the intelligence community

## Current Political Landscape (March 2026)

### DNI Tulsi Gabbard
Has promised a "fresh look" and a potential new Intelligence Community Assessment (ICA).

### CIA Director John Ratcliffe
Has pledged to review previous analysis, though Polymeropoulos notes a "creeping sense of betrayal" as visible progress remains slow.

### Criminal Referrals
In October 2025, the House Intelligence Committee sent criminal referrals to the Department of Justice regarding potential illegal actions by intelligence officials during initial investigations into AHI reports.

## Polymeropoulos's Three-Pillar Framework

### 1. Accountability
- Investigate why victims were denied care and denigrated
- Declassify and release the 2022 CIA Inspector General report
- Hold Biden-era CIA leadership accountable for "analytic bias"

### 2. Healthcare Access
- Guarantee lifelong access to Walter Reed for all victims and their families
- Use the Walter Reed model (low administrative cost, high-quality TBI care) as the standard

### 3. Attribution & Response
- Conduct a whole-of-government investigation not led solely by the CIA
- If Russia is confirmed, the U.S. must "have the courage to act, regardless of consequences"

## Key Themes and Implications

### The "Moral Injury" Framework
Polymeropoulos emphasizes that the government's active denial and derision of victims caused psychological harm comparable to the original injuries. This concept of "moral injury" — betrayal by trusted institutions — provides a powerful lens for understanding the emotional and psychological dimensions of the AHI crisis beyond physical symptoms.

### CIA as Both Victim and Obstacle
The article presents a paradox: the CIA and its personnel were attacked by a foreign adversary, but the agency's institutional response compounded the harm. Polymeropoulos does not blame the CIA rank and file but points to senior leadership and institutional culture as the source of obstruction.

### The Need for Independent Investigation
A major theme is that the CIA cannot be trusted to lead its own investigation — conflicts of interest, institutional self-protection, and reputational concerns make a whole-of-government or independent approach essential.

## Assessment: Perspective and Angle

**Supportive/Advocacy.** Polymeropoulos speaks as both a victim and a former senior intelligence officer. His dual perspective gives his account significant weight. He is not a medical or technical expert but provides a window into CIA internal culture, the victim experience within government, and the political machinations surrounding AHI. His piece is advocacy-oriented, making specific demands of the current administration. The fact that he is a CIA veteran lends credibility to his institutional analysis but also reflects his inherent position as an invested party.
