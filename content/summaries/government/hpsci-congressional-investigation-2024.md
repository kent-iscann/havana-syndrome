**Source:** House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence (HPSCI), Subcommittee on the Central Intelligence Agency
**Chairman:** Rep. Rick Crawford (R-Arkansas)
**Date:** December 5, 2024 (interim report)
**Type:** Congressional Investigation
**Ingested:** 2026-04-13

## Overview

The House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence issued a highly critical interim report on Havana Syndrome in December 2024, directly contradicting the Intelligence Community's 2023 assessment and alleging that the IC has been **"stonewalling, slow-walking, and cherry-picking"** information.

## Key Findings

### Adversary Assessment Shift
> "It is **increasingly likely** that a foreign adversary is responsible for some cases of what officials refer to as 'Anomalous Health Incidents.'" — **HPSCI Report**

This directly contradicts the ODNI's 2023 assessment that foreign adversary involvement is "very unlikely."

### Attack on 2023 IC Assessment
The committee found the 2023 Intelligence Community Assessment to be:
- **"Dubious at best, and misleading at worst"** — Rep. Rick Crawford
- Lacked "analytic integrity" and was "highly irregular in its formulation"
- Based on environmental or social factors that the committee believes are "false"
- Influenced by institutional reluctance to face "hard truths" about a failing to protect personnel

### Institutional Interference Allegations
The report accuses the Intelligence Community of:
- **"Stonewalling, slow-walking, and cherry-picking of information"** to impede the House investigation
- **"Willful malfeasance"** and a campaign to "belittle victims"
- Withholding "valuable data" from the Committee
- Hindering the investigation "to keep the truth about AHIs from Congress and, by extension, the American public"

## Key Testimony

### Greg Edgreen (Retired Army Lt. Col.)
- Ran DIA's AHI investigation (2021-2023)
- Stated the bar for proof was set **"impossibly high"**
- Victims were consistently the **"top 5-10% performing officers"** who worked against Russian interests
- Suggested the U.S. government did not want to face "hard truths" about failing to protect its citizens

### Marc Polymeropoulos (Former CIA)
- Described IC actions as a **"staggering betrayal"** and **"historic scandal"**
- Alleged the IC **"cooked the books"** to deny medical care to injured officers
- His Purple Heart was reportedly rescinded after the 2023 IC assessment

### Rep. Rick Crawford (Subcommittee Chair)
> "I have reason to believe that its claims of environmental or social factors explaining AHIs are **false**."

## Political Consequences

- Rep. Crawford recommended the removal of **Lt. Gen. Jeffrey Kruse** (former DIA Director) for his handling of an AHI survivor's case
- The Committee is demanding a **new, transparent Intelligence Community Assessment**
- A classified report with detailed findings is pending

## Significance

This investigation represents the most comprehensive **congressional challenge** to the IC's "very unlikely" assessment. It introduces:
- Direct testimony from victims and investigators
- Access to classified materials denied to other investigations
- A formal congressional finding that contradicts the IC
