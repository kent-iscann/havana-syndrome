**Ingested:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

The **Helping American Victims Afflicted by Neurological Attacks (HAVANA) Act of 2021** authorized financial support and medical care for federal personnel suffering brain injuries from Anomalous Health Incidents.

## Key Provisions

### Compensation
- **Lump-sum payment** of up to **$187,300** for qualifying brain injuries
- Additional support for ongoing medical care
- Covers current employees, former employees, and dependents

### Eligibility
- Incidents occurring on or after **January 1, 2016**
- Qualifying brain injuries from AHIs
- Covers personnel from CIA, State Department, DoD, and other federal agencies

### Legislative History
- **Unanimously passed the Senate** in June 2021
- Bipartisan sponsorship led by Senators Jeanne Shaheen (D), Susan Collins (R), and Marco Rubio (R)
- Signed by President Biden in October 2021

### Implementation Issues
- CIA began payments in August 2022
- As of July 2025, **DoD began reviewing applications**
- Senate Intelligence Committee (2024) found only **21% of CIA applicants** approved vs 67% at other agencies
- 2024 GAO report found patients confused by the process
