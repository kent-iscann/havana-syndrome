## Overview

This study examined **26 Canadian diplomats and family members** stationed in Havana who presented with neurological symptoms similar to U.S. counterparts. Using multimodal imaging (MRI, MEG) and toxicological analysis, researchers identified specific brain injuries and a likely etiology: **acquired neurotoxicity from overexposure to cholinesterase inhibitors (organophosphate and pyrethroid insecticides)**.

This stands in direct contrast to the NASEM report's chemical exposure assessment and represents a **competing hypothesis** for the cause of HAVANA Syndrome.

## Key Findings

### Primary Conclusion

> "Our findings confirm brain injury, specify the regions involved, and raise the hypothesis of overexposure to cholinesterase inhibitors as a plausible etiology." — **Friedman et al.**

### Specific Brain Pathologies

| Type | Finding | Location |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) Injury** | Significant leakage detected | Right basal forebrain and anterior insula |
| **White Matter Degradation** | Decreased fiber density | Right crus of fornix, splenium of corpus callosum |
| **Cortical Activity (MEG)** | Paroxysmal slow-wave events (PSWEs) — transient slowing <6 Hz for >5sec | Broad cortical areas |

### Cognitive Deficits
- **Spatial working memory** significantly impaired (P=0.0003)
- Pre/post data available for 8 participants (tested before and after posting)

## Toxicological Evidence

### Enzyme Activity
- Lower serum acetyl-cholinesterase (AChE) and butyryl-cholinesterase (BChE) in recently exposed individuals
- AChE and BChE are the primary targets of organophosphate neurotoxicity

### Detected Toxins
| Toxin | Detection Rate | Use Context |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **Temephos** | 60% of remotely exposed subjects | Organophosphate for mosquito larvae control in Cuba |
| **3-PBA** (pyrethroid metabolite) | 62% of exposed individuals | Common pyrethroid insecticide |

## The "Insecticide Hypothesis" — Zika Fumigation Theory

### Context
- Aggressive fumigation campaigns in Cuba during the **2016 Zika virus epidemic**
- Canadian Embassy records showed a **sharp increase in fumigation frequency** in staff residences starting January 2017

### Proposed Mechanism
- Low-dose chronic exposure to organophosphates (cholinesterase inhibitors) targets the brain's cholinergic system
- Specifically affects the **basal forebrain** and **brainstem nuclei** — consistent with observed damage

### Contrast with NASEM
- NASEM deemed chemical exposure **"unlikely"** — this study contradicts that assessment
- Key difference: NASEM had no access to the Canadian biochemical data
- NASEM noted: "No environmental samples from U.S. residences showed OPs at the time of testing" — but OPs degrade quickly

## Clinical & Public Health Implications

### Key Data: Persistent Symptoms Prevalence

| Symptom | Recent Exposure (%) | Remote Exposure (%) | P Value |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|---------|
| Headaches | 54% | 91% | <0.000 |
| Tinnitus | 18% | 66% | 0.001 |
| Fatigue | 45% | 66% | 0.002 |
| Concentration issues | 54% | 33% | 0.014 |

**Note:** Remote exposure (tested 14 months post-return) showed **worse** headache rates — suggesting possible progressive damage or cumulative effects.

### Actionable Insights
1. **Diagnostic Biomarkers:** DCE-MRI (BBB leakage) and MEG (PSWEs) are effective research tools for identifying atypical brain injuries missed by standard MRIs
2. **Partial Reversibility:** BBB leakage and MEG abnormalities were less pronounced in remotely exposed individuals — suggests potential partial recovery once exposure stops
3. **Prevention:** Ban organophosphate insecticides; use non-neurotoxic alternatives for pest control

## Study Limitations
- **Small cohort** — 26 subjects
- **Preprint status** — not peer-reviewed at time of posting
- **Causality** — correlation with insecticides is strong, but other sources of organophosphates (including nerve agents) cannot be definitively ruled out
- Canadian cohort lacked the acute directional auditory/pressure symptoms reported by U.S. personnel (NASEM noted this discrepancy)
