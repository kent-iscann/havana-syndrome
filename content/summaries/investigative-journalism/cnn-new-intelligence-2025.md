## Overview

CNN reported on a new unclassified ODNI summary revealing a **split in the intelligence community's assessment** of Havana Syndrome. While the majority maintained its "very unlikely" stance, two agencies shifted for the first time to acknowledge that a foreign weapon is possible.

## Key Findings

### Intelligence Community Split
- **Two agencies** assessed a **"roughly even chance"** a foreign country used a novel weapon
- **Two agencies** assessed a **"roughly even chance"** a foreign actor *possesses* such capability but hasn't deployed it
- **Majority** still "very unlikely" a foreign actor is responsible
- All assessments at **low confidence**

### IC's Evidence
> "All IC components agree that years of IC collection, targeting and analytic efforts have not surfaced compelling intelligence reporting that ties a foreign actor to any specific event."

- Intelligence collection shows **credible adversaries denying involvement** in private communications
- Lab work on RF bioeffects showing "mixed results" (previous studies showed none)

### Political Reaction
- **House Intelligence Committee:** Issued report finding it **"increasingly likely"** a foreign adversary was responsible
- **Mark Lenzi** (former State Department officer, AHI victim): *"This see-no-evil ridiculous assessment not only covers for Russia but proves the point of the recent HPSCI report."*
- Victims feel "gaslighted" by IC assessments
- IC officials insist they are not "pulling punches"

### Current Status
- Injuries acknowledged as real regardless of cause
- Biden administration planning briefings for incoming Trump team
- Focus on interdisciplinary work combining government expertise with outside specialists
