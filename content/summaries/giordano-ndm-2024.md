## Overview

Dr. James Giordano, who served as a lead forensic evaluator for the original 2016 Havana cases, argues that directed energy remains the **primary suspect** for Anomalous Health Incidents. His assessment combines clinical evaluations, forensic review, and technology analysis.

## Clinical Evidence

### Subjective Symptoms Reported
- Sensations of intense head pressure
- Spatial, perceptual, and cognitive disorientation
- Inability to focus and profound fatigue
- Vertigo

### Objective Clinical Signs
- Balance and coordination dysfunction
- Diminished short-, intermediate-, and long-term episodic and procedural memory
- Impairment of fine and gross motor abilities
- Neurocognitive abnormalities (vigilance, task focus)

## Primary Suspects

### 1. Ultrasonic Stimulation
- **High probability** based on clinical presentation and environmental ecological data
- Could be focused directionally and produce effects consistent with victim reports

### 2. Pulsed Microwaves
- **High probability** consistent with the "Frye Effect" (auditory sensations from microwave pulses)
- Known technological capabilities in 2016
- Could be focused on one room without affecting adjacent rooms

> "It was—and remains—unclear whether exposure to an ultrasonic and/or microwave stimulus occurred singularly, in some combination, or in tandem."

## NASEM Corroboration

Giordano's 2017 findings were later corroborated by the 2020 NASEM report:
> "The committee found a constellation of acute clinical signs and symptoms with directional locational specific features that was distinctive to its knowledge. This constellation of clinical features is unlike any disorder in neurological or general medical literature."

## Technology Status

- Technologies required were **"extant, scalable, fieldable, and in operational use"** by 2016
- Evidence of R&D by both **transatlantic and transpacific near-peer competitor nations**
- Intent may have been collateral damage (from bugging equipment) or intentional anti-personnel deployment

## Conclusions

- Critical need to monitor **dual-use** nature of emerging biotechnologies
- Need for better response protocols for personnel exposed to energy signatures
- AHIs represent a **"clear and present risk"** requiring shift in political, intelligence, and military postures
