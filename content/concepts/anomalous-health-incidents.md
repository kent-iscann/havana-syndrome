## Overview

**Anomalous Health Incidents (AHIs)** is the official U.S. government term for what is commonly known as **Havana Syndrome**. The term encompasses a range of unexplained neurological symptoms reported by U.S. and allied government personnel worldwide since 2016.

## Definition

The U.S. intelligence community defines AHIs as incidents characterized by:
- Acute onset of neurological symptoms
- Perceived localized sounds or sensations
- Lasting cognitive, vestibular, or auditory dysfunction
- No immediately identifiable environmental or medical cause

## Scale

- First reported: Late 2016, U.S. Embassy Havana
- **~1,500** Americans reported AHIs globally (as of 2024)
- **334** qualified for military health system care (as of July 2024)

## Geographic Spread

Havana (Cuba), Guangzhou (China), Vienna (Austria), Washington D.C., Moscow, Berlin, Vietnam, India, Serbia, Colombia, Georgia, Poland, Australia, Taiwan, and many more.

## Government Response

- **HAVANA Act (2021):** PL 117-46 — authorized compensation up to ~$187,300 for qualifying brain injuries
- **Form 244:** DoD acute assessment protocol
- **ODNI assessments:** 2023 and 2025 intelligence community evaluations
- **Senate Intelligence Committee (2024):** Found CIA mishandled AHI cases

## Causes Under Debate

1. **Directed energy weapons** (microwave, ultrasonic, laser) — supported by NASEM, Giordano
2. **Mass psychogenic illness** — proposed by FBI Behavioral Analysis Unit
3. **Environmental factors** — crickets, HVAC, surveillance equipment
4. **Pre-existing medical conditions** — cited by ODNI for majority of cases
