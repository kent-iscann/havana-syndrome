## Overview

The optoacoustic effect, proposed in **2026** by a **Czech Academy of Sciences fellow**, suggests that **pulsed infrared lasers** can generate acoustic effects in tissue — particularly in the inner ear — producing symptoms consistent with AHIs.

## Mechanism

Short pulses of infrared laser light are absorbed by tissue, causing rapid thermal expansion that generates acoustic waves. In the inner ear, this can produce:
- Vertigo and balance disruption
- Tinnitus and perceived sounds
- Nausea and disorientation
- Head pressure

## Advantages (from attacker's perspective)

- **Portability:** Infrared lasers can be miniaturized to portable size
- **Silent:** No external acoustic signature
- **Non-heating:** Pulses are too brief to cause thermal damage
- **Directional:** Can be focused precisely
- **Hard to detect:** No RF emissions to detect with standard equipment

## Status

- Proposed in 2026 — recent addition to AHI debate
- Not yet experimentally proven to cause AHI-like symptoms
- Part of the 2026 wave of new evidence (alongside RF device acquisition by DHS)
