## Overview

The blood-brain barrier (BBB) is a selective semipermeable border that separates circulating blood from brain tissue. BBB injury allows potentially harmful substances to pass from blood into brain tissue, causing inflammation and neurological damage.

## Evidence in AHIs

### Dalhousie Study (2019)
- Canadian diplomats in Havana showed **significant BBB leakage** on DCE-MRI imaging
- Affected regions: **right basal forebrain** and **anterior insula**
- BBB leakage was **less pronounced** in remotely tested individuals (14 months post-return) — suggesting partial recovery once exposure stops
- **Key finding:** Standard structural MRIs (the type most commonly used clinically) do not detect BBB leakage — DCE-MRI is required

### Implications for NIH Studies
- The 2024 NIH JAMA studies found no significant MRI-detectable brain injury
- However, NIH used **standard structural MRI** — not the specialized DCE-MRI needed to detect BBB injury
- This raises questions about whether the NIH studies could have missed BBB-related pathology

## Detection Methods

| Method | Detects BBB Injury? | Standard Clinical Use? |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Standard Structural MRI | **No** | Yes |
| DCE-MRI (Dynamic Contrast-Enhanced) | **Yes** | No — research tool |
| MEG (Magnetoencephalography) | Indirectly (via PSWEs) | No — research tool |
