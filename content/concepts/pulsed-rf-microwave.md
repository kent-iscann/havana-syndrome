## Overview

Pulsed radiofrequency (RF) and microwave radiation are forms of electromagnetic energy delivered in brief, discrete pulses rather than continuous waveforms. The pulsing characteristic is critical to their biological effects.

## Relevance to AHIs

### NASEM Assessment (2020)
- Identified as the **"most plausible mechanism"** for acute AHI symptoms
- Frequency range 0.4-3 GHz can induce the **Fry Effect** — audible sounds inside the head without external source
- Can be focused directionally on specific locations/rooms
- Non-thermal effects (symptoms without heating sensation) possible through cell membrane dysfunction or voltage-gated calcium channel disruption

### Clinical Consistency
Acute symptoms consistent with pulsed RF exposure:
- **Auditory phenomena** — clicking, buzzing, chirping (Fry Effect)
- **Head pressure/vibration** — thermal expansion of tissue
- **Directional effects** — focused beams can target specific rooms
- **Immediate relief on relocation** — suggests physical force penetrating windows not walls
- **Chronic effects** — headache, fatigue, dizziness, cognitive deficits

### Russian/Soviet Research
- Documented that **pulsing** is the most critical factor in determining biological effects
- Decades of Western and Soviet literature support biological effects of pulsed RF
- Technologies were "extant, scalable, fieldable, in operational use" by 2016

### Challenging Evidence
- JASON report (2018/2022): Microwave/RF causes "highly unlikely"
- NIH studies (2024): No MRI-detectable brain injury (though standard MRI may not detect RF effects)
