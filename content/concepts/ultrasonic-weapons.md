**Updated:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

Ultrasonic weapons use directional sound at frequencies above the threshold of human hearing (>20 kHz) to produce physical effects at a distance.

## Effects

- Vestibular disruption (vertigo, imbalance)
- Nausea and disorientation
- Head pressure and pain
- Cognitive impairment at sufficient intensity

## Evidence in AHIs

### Supporting
- Giordano's 2017 forensic evaluation identified ultrasonic stimulation as **high probability** alongside pulsed microwaves
- Could explain both the auditory phenomena and the vestibular/cognitive symptoms
- Directionality matches victim reports of room-specific effects

### Challenging
- JASON report (2018) concluded audio recordings were most likely **cricket sounds** (not ultrasound)
- Ultrasound dissipates more rapidly over distance than microwaves
