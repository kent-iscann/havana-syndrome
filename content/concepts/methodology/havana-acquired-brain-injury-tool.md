## Overview

The HABIT (HAVANA Acquired Brain Injury Tool) is a clinical screening tool developed by the U.S. State Department's Bureau of Medical Services to assess neuro-cognitive symptoms in personnel returning from Cuba.

## Development and Use
- Developed as a rapid assessment protocol for embassy personnel
- Designed to identify the specific constellation of symptoms associated with AHIs
- Used to screen returning diplomats and determine need for further evaluation

## Evaluation

### CDC Assessment (2019)
- CDC evaluated HABIT as part of its Cuba Unexplained Events Investigation
- Found it **insufficient for determining causation**
- Tool identified symptoms but did not help differentiate between possible causes

### NASEM Assessment (2020)
- NASEM recommended updating HABIT to include **broader range of symptoms**
- Recommended adding epidemiological data collection (physical location, environmental parameters at onset)
- Recommended it be used as part of a **comprehensive pre-post deployment screening** protocol

## Recommendations

NASEM recommended:
- **Baseline testing** for all personnel before deployment
- **Longitudinal follow-up** with standardized tools
- HABIT should include environmental parameters alongside clinical symptoms
