## Overview

Epidemiological investigation is the systematic study of the distribution, patterns, and determinants of health events in defined populations. It is the core methodology used by the CDC in its Cuba Unexplained Events Investigation.

## Application to AHIs

### CDC Approach (2019)
- Reviewed 95 individual cases (15 presumptive, 31 possible, 49 ruled out)
- Attempted to identify a mechanism, exposure process, effective treatment, or mitigating factor
- Evaluated competing theories: directed energy, chemical, infectious, psychogenic
- All returned negative — no single mechanism identified

### Methodological Challenges
- No control group (no data on asymptomatic personnel in same environment)
- HIPAA regulations prevented access to individual-level health data
- Delayed start (CDC began 1 year after first events)
- Patients evaluated at vastly different intervals post-onset
- Diverse patient population (age, medical history, career role, geographic location)

### CDC Framework
The CDC followed its standard cluster investigation framework:
1. **Initial contact** — collect information from reporters
2. **Assessment** — determine if cases are above expected rates
3. **Feasibility study** — examine potential for epidemiologic study
4. **Etiologic investigation** — determine disease-exposure relationship
