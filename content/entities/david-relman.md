## Overview

David A. Relman, MD, of Stanford University, led the **National Academy of Sciences committee** on Anomalous Health Incidents. His 2020 report remains one of the most authoritative scientific assessments of the Havana Syndrome mystery.

## Key Finding

The NASEM report (December 2020) deemed **pulsed radiofrequency energy** the "most plausible" explanation for a subset of cases:

> "The most plausible mechanism... was directed, pulsed radiofrequency energy... it could be focused on one room and not another room in the same house."

> "The committee found a constellation of acute clinical signs and symptoms with directional locational specific features that was distinctive to its knowledge. This constellation of clinical features is unlike any disorder in neurological or general medical literature."

## Significance

Relman's NASEM committee findings have been cited repeatedly by the directed-energy proponent camp (including Giordano) and remain the primary scientific endorsement of the directed energy hypothesis at the national academy level.
