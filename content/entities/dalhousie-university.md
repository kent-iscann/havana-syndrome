## Overview

Dalhousie University, in collaboration with the University of Pennsylvania, conducted the study that proposed the **organophosphate neurotoxicity hypothesis** for Canadian diplomats affected in Havana.

## Friedman et al. Study (2019)

### Key Findings
- **26 Canadian diplomats** and family members examined
- **Blood-brain barrier leakage** detected in right basal forebrain and anterior insula
- **White matter degradation** — decreased fiber density in specific structures
- **Paroxysmal slow-wave events** on MEG
- **Toxicological evidence:** Temephos (organophosphate) detected in 60% of remotely exposed; 3-PBA (pyrethroid metabolite) in 62%
- Pre/post comparison data for 8 participants (tested before and after posting)

### Contrast with U.S. Findings
- Canadian cohort **lacked the acute directional auditory/pressure symptoms** reported by U.S. personnel
- Symptoms developed **gradually** rather than acutely
- This difference led NASEM to note that the Canadian cases may have **different causes** from the U.S. cases
