## Overview

The State Department was the first U.S. government agency to encounter Anomalous Health Incidents and was later found to have mishandled the crisis response.

## Role in AHI Events

### Embassy Havana Response
- First reports of AHIs emerged from U.S. Embassy personnel in Havana (late 2016)
- Emergency Action Committee meetings began April 2017
- In September 2017, Secretary Rex Tillerson ordered a **60% reduction in embassy staff** and closure of the Consulate — **without formal risk/benefit analysis**

### ARB Findings (June 2018)
The Accountability Review Board issued a damning assessment:
- **"Systemic disorganization"** and **lack of senior leadership**
- No senior official designated with overall responsibility — the "single most significant deficiency"
- ARB only convened in February 2018, months after escalation, and only after **political pressure from Senator Marco Rubio**
- State Department **blocked CDC research for nearly a year** citing privacy concerns

### Compensation
- State Department designated AHIs as qualifying for HAVANA Act compensation
- Provides financial support up to ~$187,000 per qualifying injury
