## Overview

The CDC conducted the primary federal epidemiological investigation into Anomalous Health Incidents in Cuba (2016–2019). Despite its comprehensive scope, the investigation was unable to identify any cause.

## Cuba Unexplained Events Investigation (2019)

### Timeline
- **December 2017:** State Dept formally requested CDC inquiry
- **November 2018:** State Dept finally provided **unredacted medical records** — **11-month delay**
- **December 2019:** CDC completed final report — marked "For Official Use Only"
- **January 2021:** Report released via FOIA to National Security Archive

### Findings — Inconclusive
- **No mechanism of injury identified**
- **No process of exposure identified**
- **No effective treatment or mitigating factor identified**
- All evaluated theories (directed energy, chemical, infectious, psychogenic) returned negative findings

> "The evaluations conducted thus far have not identified a mechanism of injury, process of exposure, effective treatment, or mitigating factor..."

### The HABIT Protocol
CDC evaluated the **Havana Acquired Brain Injury Tool (HABIT)** — the State Department's clinical screening tool for neuro-cognitive symptoms — but found it insufficient for determining causation.

## Obstruction Issues
- State Department lawyers initially blocked CDC from accessing medical data citing privacy concerns
- CDC investigation started a year after first events — too late for optimal data collection
- Report held as "For Official Use Only" for over a year
