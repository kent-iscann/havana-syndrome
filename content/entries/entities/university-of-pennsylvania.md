# University of Pennsylvania — Clinical Study on Havana Syndrome

**Type:** Organization — Academic Medical Center
**Updated:** 2026-04-13

## Overview

The University of Perelman School of Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania published a clinical study (Swanson et al., 2018) that was one of the first to provide neuroimaging evidence of brain changes in Havana Syndrome patients.

## 2018 Study (Swanson et al.)

### Key Findings
- Found evidence of **injury to widespread brain networks** resembling persistent concussion
- Group differences in brain volume and connectivity compared to healthy controls
- Published alongside **criticism in Cortex journal** citing "gross methodological flaws"

### NASEM Assessment
- Study was referenced in the NASEM 2020 report
- Committee noted findings "suggested group differences in brain volume and connectivity, but lacked a replication cohort"
- Deemed **insufficient for individual diagnosis**
- NIH 2024 studies **failed to replicate** these findings
