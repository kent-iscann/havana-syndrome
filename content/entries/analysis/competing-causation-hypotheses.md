# Competing Causation Hypotheses

**Type:** Analysis — Comparative Theory Assessment
**Updated:** 2026-04-13

## Overview

The central question of Havana Syndrome — what caused the reported symptoms — has produced multiple competing hypotheses. This page systematically compares them, assesses their evidence, and tracks which institutions support which view.

## Hypothesis Comparison Table

| Hypothesis | Proponent(s) | Evidence Strength | Key Weakness |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Directed RF Energy** | NASEM, Giordano, 60 Minutes | Strongest clinical consistency | No direct intelligence evidence |
| **Organophosphate Neurotoxicity** | Dalhousie (Friedman et al.) | Strong biochemical evidence for Canadian cohort | U.S. cases had different acute symptoms |
| **Mass Psychogenic Illness** | FBI BAU, Bartholomew & Baloh | High-stress environment, cluster pattern | Cannot explain acute directional/locational onset |

| **Environmental/HVAC** | CIA interim report (2022) | Some cases plausibly environmental | Cannot explain global pattern |
| **Ultrasonic/Acoustic Weapons** | Giordano, 60 Minutes | Plausible mechanism, Unit 29155 acoustic weapons bonus | Hard to prove vs RF at a distance |

## Detailed Hypothesis Assessment

### 1. Directed RF Energy — **Most Plausible for Acute U.S. Cases**

**Support:** NASEM (2020), Giordano (2017, 2024), DNI Scientific Panel (2022), 60 Minutes investigation

**Evidence:**
- Acute symptoms match known RF effects (Fry Effect for auditory, non-thermal)
- Directional and room-specific features consistent with focused beam
- Immediate relief upon moving to different room suggests physical force penetrating windows
- Technologies were extant and operational by 2016
- GRU Unit 29155 found to have acoustic weapons development program (2024 accounting document)
- U.S. Homeland Security acquired backpack-sized RF device for testing (2026)

**Gaps:**
- No intercepted communications linking foreign actor to specific incidents
- NIH studies found no MRI-detectable brain injury (though standard MRI may not detect RF effects)
- Private intelligence shows adversaries denying involvement

---

### 2. Organophosphate Neurotoxicity — **Plausible for Canadian Cases**

**Support:** Dalhousie University (Friedman et al., 2019)

**Evidence:**
- Biochemical evidence: lower AChE/BChE enzyme activity in exposed individuals
- Toxicological evidence: Temephos detected in 60% of remotely exposed
- BBB leakage on DCE-MRI — specific pathology
- White matter degradation confirmed by advanced imaging
- Temporal correlation with aggressive Zika fumigation campaign starting January 2017
- Pre/post data for 8 participants (tested before and after posting)

**Gaps:**
- Canadian cohort lacked acute directional auditory symptoms that U.S. personnel reported
- NASEM deemed chemical exposure "unlikely" — no environmental samples from U.S. residences showed OPs
- OPs degrade quickly — absence at testing time doesn't prove absence at exposure time
- U.S. clinical history not consistent with classic cholinergic crisis

**Assessment:** Most plausible for the Canadian cohort, but **less applicable to U.S. cases** which had distinctive acute-onset features not consistent with chemical exposure.

---

### 3. Mass Psychogenic Illness — **Possible Secondary Factor**

**Support:** FBI Behavioral Analysis Unit (2018), Bartholomew & Baloh

**Evidence:**
- High-stress embassy environment
- Cluster pattern of symptom reporting
- PPPD (functional disorder) diagnosed in ~25% of NIH patients — demonstrates how initial physical insult can trigger persistent functional symptoms via neurological adaptation

**Gaps:**
- Cannot explain acute directional symptoms (sound from specific direction, immediate relief in different room)
- Does not account for biochemical findings (OP levels, BBB leakage)
- Global pattern across multiple countries with no social contact between cases
- NASEM: MPI cannot explain the initial sudden-onset audio-vestibular signs

**Assessment:** May explain symptom spread and chronic persistence (via PPPD), but **cannot be the primary cause** of acute onset.

---

### 4. Infectious Disease — **Highly Unlikely**

**Support:** Cuban Academy of Sciences (2021)

**Evidence:**
- Zika epidemic was epidemic in Cuba during 2016-2017
- Zika known to cause neurological complications (Guillain-Barré, vestibular disease) in 0.1-0.2% of cases

**Gaps:**
- Zika symptoms include fever, rash, joint pain — **absent** in all AHI cohorts
- Sudden onset inconsistent with infectious disease incubation period
- NASEM deemed "highly unlikely" — clinical constellation does not match Zika

**Assessment:** Effectively ruled out as primary cause.

---

### 5. Insect Sounds (Cricket) — **Partial Explanation at Best**

**Support:** JASON Advisory Group (2018/2022)

**Evidence:**
- Indies short-tailed cricket calls match frequency and pattern of audio recordings made by Havana victims
- Cricket species exists in Cuba

**Gaps:**
- Cricket not endemic to all attack locations (Vienna, Berlin, Guangzhou, etc.)
- Does not explain broader symptoms beyond auditory experience
- Room-specific localization of sounds inconsistent with insect behavior
- Multiple AHI victims dispute cricket as sole explanation

**Assessment:** May explain the audio recordings in Cuba specifically, but **does not explain the global phenomenon**.

---

## Conclusion: Different Causes for Different Cohorts?

The evidence increasingly suggests that **AHIs may not have a single cause**:

1. **U.S. Embassy Havana** — Most consistent with directed RF energy (acute onset, directional, Fry Effect symptoms)
2. **Canadian Embassy Havana** — Most consistent with organophosphate neurotoxicity (gradual onset, BBB leakage, OP detection, lack of acute auditory symptoms)
3. **Global cases post-2017** — Pattern consistent with directed energy (clustering at specific locations, temporal correlation with GRU travel)
4. **Chronic symptoms across all cohorts** — May include PPPD component (functional disorder triggered by initial insult)

This "multiple causes" hypothesis would explain why no single investigation has found a universal answer — **different cohorts may have genuinely different causes**.
