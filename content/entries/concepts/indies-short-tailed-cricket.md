# Indies Short-Tailed Cricket — Insect Sound Hypothesis

**Type:** Concept — Environmental / Natural Explanation
**Updated:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

The Indies short-tailed cricket (*Anurogryllus celerinictus*) is a species whose calls match the audio recordings made by Havana Syndrome victims in Cuba. This is the leading natural/environmental explanation for the "strange sounds."

## Evidence

### JASON Advisory Group (2018/2022)
- Independent scientific advisory group concluded the "strange sounds" were **most likely** the Indies short-tailed cricket
- Cricket calls match the frequency and pattern of the sounds recorded by victims

### Challenging Evidence
- Victims reported sounds in **locations where this cricket doesn't exist** (Vienna, Berlin, Guangzhou, etc.)
- Does not explain the broader constellation of symptoms beyond auditory experience
- Room-specific localization of sounds is inconsistent with insect behavior
- Many AHI victims dispute the cricket explanation as reducing a complex phenomenon to a trivial coincidence
