# Mass Psychogenic Illness

**Type:** Concept — Psychological / Social Mechanism
**Updated:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

Mass psychogenic illness (MPI) — also called mass hysteria or collective delusion — is a psychological mechanism where genuine physical symptoms spread among groups through suggestion, stress, and social contagion. In the Havana Syndrome context, this is proposed as an **alternative explanation** to directed energy weapons.

## Application to AHIs

### Proponents
- **FBI Behavioral Analysis Unit** (2018) proposed MPI as explanation
- Researchers **Robert Bartholomew** and **Robert W. Baloh** advocate "moral panic" / "nocebo effect" theory
- **Cuban Academy of Sciences** (2021) dismissed "mysterious syndrome" narrative in favor of psychogenic explanations

### Mechanism
- Stress and fear of targeted attack manifest as authentic physical symptoms
- "Nocebo effect" — expectation of harm produces actual harm
- Symptoms are real, not faked, but the cause is psychological rather than environmental

### Criticism
- Many victims and researchers **vehemently reject** MPI characterization as dismissive and harmful
- Directional and room-specific nature of symptoms is difficult to explain via MPI
- NASEM found a "constellation of acute clinical signs and symptoms with directional locational specific features that was **distinctive** to its knowledge"
- MPI explanation does not account for the temporal clustering at specific diplomatic postings
