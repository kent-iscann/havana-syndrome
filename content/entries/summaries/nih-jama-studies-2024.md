# NIH Clinical Studies — No Detectable Brain Injury in Havana Syndrome Patients

**Source:** National Institutes of Health (NIH) / JAMA
**Date:** March 18 2024
**Type:** Peer-Reviewed Clinical Studies
**Ingested:** 2026-04-06
**Status:** Studies later shut down due to coercion concerns

## Overview

NIH published two studies in JAMA following a five-year clinical investigation of AHI patients. The studies found **no significant differences** in brain structure or biomarkers between AHI patients and control groups, failing to replicate earlier University of Pennsylvania findings.

## Key Findings

### Brain Imaging and Assessment
- **~80 current and former U.S. officials** examined through brain imaging and clinical assessments
- **No significant differences** in brain structure or function between AHI patients and controls
- **No MRI-detectable brain injury** or biological abnormalities found
- Failed to **replicate 2018 UPenn study** findings of "widespread brain network" injury

### What NIH Did Find
- Higher rates of **imbalance, fatigue, post-traumatic stress, and depression** among AHI reporters
- Severe symptoms acknowledged despite lack of detectable biomarkers

### Study Shutdown
- Studies were **subsequently shut down** after an internal investigation revealed some participants had been **coerced to join**
- This has been cited by critics as further undermining the NIH findings
