# Global Affairs Canada — Unexplained Health Incidents Investigation

**Source:** Global Affairs Canada (GAC), Canadian Government
**Date:** August 2024 (final report); 2017-2024 (investigation period)
**Type:** Government Investigation Report
**Ingested:** 2026-04-13

## Overview

Global Affairs Canada (GAC) issued its final report in August 2024 on the Unexplained Health Incidents (UHI) that affected Canadian diplomats in Havana. After seven years of multi-agency investigation, GAC concluded that the incidents were **"not the result of a malicious act by a foreign actor."**

## Key Conclusions

> "GAC... has concluded that the UHI were not the result of a malicious act of a foreign actor or foreign actors and that pre-existing medical conditions, environmental factors and conventional illnesses were likely to have been important factors in many of the symptoms experienced." — **GAC Final Report**

### Official Position
- **No criminality** found by RCMP or CSIS
- **No foreign interference** identified
- **Symptoms acknowledged as authentic** — "authenticity of the symptoms they experienced has never been questioned"
- File transitioning from security task force to **occupational health and safety** matter

## Investigation Components

### 1. Dalhousie University / Nova Scotia Health Authority Study (2018-2024)
- **26 participants** monitored from 2018 to 2024
- Approximately **half experienced symptoms** associated with mild brain injury
- Symptoms typically developed within **first 6 months of assignment**
- Hypothesized link to **organophosphate pesticides** found in mass fumigation

### 2. Golder Associates Environmental Assessment (November 2019)
- Commissioned to verify the pesticide hypothesis
- **Analyzed:** soil, food, air, water, textiles
- **Conclusion:** Health risks related to pesticides found at **"acceptable levels"**
- *"There were no obvious hazards identified as likely sources of CBS health issues."*

### 3. RCMP Criminal Investigation (Project Spectre)
- Led by RCMP's Integrated National Security Enforcement Team (INSET)
- **Monitoring instruments** installed in staff quarters 2019-2022
- Detected: acoustic/radiation surges, ozone, humidity, other environmental factors
- **Results:** "Data provided no probative information to identify a cause"

## Contrast with Canadian Study Findings

### Dalhousie University Study (Friedman et al., 2019)
- Found **acquired neurotoxicity** from organophosphate/pyrethroid exposure
- **Blood-brain barrier leakage** detected
- **Lower enzyme activity** (AChE, BChE) in exposed individuals
- Toxicological evidence: Temephos in 60% of remotely exposed; 3-PBA in 62%

### GAC Final Report (2024)
- Golder Associates found no significant environmental pesticide risks
- RCMP found no criminal or foreign interference evidence
- Shift from "security threat" to "occupational health matter"
- **Acknowledges** Dalhousie findings but does not adopt them as definitive

### The Tension
- The Dalhousie study provided the **best evidence for chemical exposure** as a cause
- But GAC's own environmental assessment (Golder) found **no significant hazards**
- The gap may reflect **timing** — pesticides degrade quickly; Golder tested in 2019, fumigation peaked 2016-2017

## Political Fallout

### Domestic Controversy
- Paul Miller (lawyer representing 17 plaintiffs) accused Ottawa of **downplaying "Havana Syndrome"** and dismissing claims
- Global Affairs Canada staff at one point discussed whether symptoms were **imagined**
- Canadian staff bound for Cuba were told to **stay silent** about the syndrome

### Response to U.S. Intelligence Shift
When U.S. intelligence assessments shifted against foreign adversary involvement in 2023, GAC's 2024 report cited this as supporting its own conclusion. Miller criticized this, arguing the U.S. intelligence assessments had **"created distrust"** and **"caused real, serious harm to some of our nation's bravest."**
