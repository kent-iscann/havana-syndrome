# National Security Archive — Secrets of the Havana Syndrome

**Source:** National Security Archive, George Washington University
**Author/Editor:** Peter Kornbluh
**Date:** February 10 2021
**Type:** Digital Document Collection / Briefing Book
**Ingested:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

The National Security Archive published a comprehensive briefing book compiling declassified documents related to Havana Syndrome. This was the first public release of the heavily redacted Accountability Review Board (ARB) report (June 7 2018, SECRET/NOFORN), obtained through a FOIA request.

## Key Content

### Declassified ARB Report Findings
- Trump administration's response plagued by **"systemic disorganization"** and lack of senior leadership
- Tillerson State Department failed to conduct risk/benefit assessment before reducing embassy staff
- CIA confirmed withdrawal of Havana station — previously undisclosed
- Two CIA TDY personnel injured at Hotel Nacional in August 2017 (confirmed by ARB)
- First official confirmation of cases in China (Guangzhou), Tashkent, and one other **redacted country**

### Timeline Established

### CDC Research Blocked
- State Dept commissioned CDC to investigate in late 2017
- State lawyers blocked study for **nearly a year** citing privacy restrictions
- Impasse resolved November 2018 only after ARB recommendation

### Critical Quotes
> "The Department of State's response to these incidents was characterized by a lack of senior leadership, ineffective communications, and systemic disorganization." — ARB Executive Summary

> "The mechanism for the cause of the injuries is currently unknown. We do not know the motive, when they commenced, or who did it." — ARB Report
