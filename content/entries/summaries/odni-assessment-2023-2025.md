# ODNI Assessment — Intelligence Community Evaluation of AHIs

**Source:** Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI)
**Date:** February 2023 (original), January 2025 (update summary)
**Type:** Intelligence Community Assessment
**Ingested:** 2026-04-06

## Overview

The ODNI's assessment represents the most comprehensive official intelligence community position on Anomalous Health Incidents. It aggregates judgments from all seven U.S. intelligence agencies involved.

## 2023 Assessment (Original)

### Consensus Finding
- **All seven agencies** concluded that foreign adversary involvement is **"very unlikely"** to be responsible
- **"No credible evidence"** found linking a foreign actor to any specific AHI event
- The vast majority of reported cases attributed to natural causes or pre-existing medical conditions
- **"Many unanswered questions"** remain due to research gaps

### Key Statements
> "All IC components agree that years of IC collection, targeting and analytic efforts have not surfaced compelling intelligence reporting that ties a foreign actor to any specific event reported as a possible AHI."

## 2025 Update (Shift in Assessments)

### New Intelligence
- Intelligence indicates foreign nations' **directed energy research programs** have been **"making progress"**
- A subset of cases may involve capabilities not previously recognized

### Split in Agency Assessments
- **Five agencies** maintained the "very unlikely" stance on foreign adversary involvement
- **Two agencies** shifted their assessments (with low confidence):
  - **Agency A (reportedly NSA):** Assessed a **"roughly even chance"** that a foreign country used a novel weapon against U.S. personnel
  - **Agency B:** Assessed a **"roughly even chance"** that a foreign actor *possesses* such capability but is unlikely to have deployed it

### Confidence Levels
- Both minority judgments made with **"low confidence"**
- No agency has high confidence in any assessment on foreign involvement

## Contradictory Evidence
- **Private denials:** Intelligence collection shows credible adversaries expressing surprise or denying involvement
- **Scientific testing:** IC supporting lab work on RF bioeffects; recent limited studies show "mixed results" (previous studies showed no effects)
- **Expert panel:** Previous panel judged directed energy (pulsed electromagnetic/acoustic) most plausible for subset of cases
