# Treatments and Recovery — AHI Victim Care

**Type:** Analysis Page — Medical Treatments/Outcomes
**Updated:** 2026-04-13

## Overview

Despite the unresolved cause of Anomalous Health Incidents, significant work has been done on treatment and rehabilitation for affected personnel. This page covers medical treatments, government-recognized care protocols, and the debate over classifying AHIs as combat injuries.

## Approved Treatments

### Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)
- Some AHI victims have been **successfully treated with HBOT** at DoD facilities like the National Intrepid Center of Excellence (NICoE)
- HBOT has demonstrated **statistically significant symptomatic and cognitive improvements** in mild TBI patients
- TreatNow.org reports HBOT can end suicidal ideation, reduce PTSD symptoms, and help heal brain wounds

#### DOD-Sponsored HBOT Studies
| Study | Protocol | Control | Key Findings |
|-------|----------|---------|--------------|
| **Air Force (N=50)** | 2.4 ATA, 100% O2, 30 sessions | Sham (1.3 ATA) | No significant differences vs sham |
| **Navy/DARPA (N=60)** | 2.0 or 1.5 ATA, 40 sessions | Sham (room air) | No significant benefits over sham |
| **Army HOPPS (N=72)** | 1.5 ATA, 40 sessions | Sham (1.2 ATA) or no HBOT | Mixed results |

**Note:** Despite mixed controlled trial results, many victims report subjective improvement with HBOT.

### Vestibular Rehabilitation
- **Early evaluation** and vestibular therapy recommended by NASEM
- Multi-disciplinary plan (physical + psychological therapy + medication) over **3-6 months** typically reduces PPPD symptoms to non-impaired level

### PPPD Treatment
- 10-20% of patients with PPPD for 4+ years remain **work-disabled** despite maximal treatment
- Patients remain susceptible to temporary symptom exacerbations from provocative stimuli (motion-rich environments)

## The Purple Heart Debate

### Core Issue
To receive the **Purple Heart**, an injury must be officially linked to **"enemy action."** The IC's 2023 conclusion that foreign adversary involvement is "very unlikely" became the primary barrier to military honors.

### Key Cases
- **Marc Polymeropoulos (former CIA):** Purple Heart was **approved but rescinded** after the 2023 IC assessment was released
- **"Chris" (retired Lt. Col.):** 
  - Attacked 5 times at home in Northern Virginia in 2020
  - Lost 50% hearing in left ear, permanent organ system damage
  - VA classified as "100%, permanent and total" disabled
  - Purple Heart application blocked
  - Suspects Russia due to his classified satellite program work
- **Army reportedly issued and then canceled** a Purple Heart for a Walter Reed patient after the 2023 IC assessment

### Political Response
- Rep. Rick Crawford recommended removal of **Lt. Gen. Jeffrey Kruse** (former DIA Director) for handling an AHI survivor's case
- HPSCI report called for new assessment to enable proper recognition of victims

## Government Care Access

### HAVANA Act Implementation
- Lump-sum payment of up to **$187,300** for qualifying brain injuries
- Senate report (2024): Only **21% of CIA applicants** approved vs 67% at other agencies
- CIA contested claims more aggressively than other agencies
- GAO (2024) found patients confused by the care-seeking process

### Form 244
- DoD acute assessment protocol for AHI reporting
- Designed to streamline evaluation but GAO found gaps in implementation

## Treatment Gap Analysis

| Need | Status |
