## Victim Selection Pattern

**Greg Edgreen** (retired DIA Lt. Col.) revealed a critical targeting pattern:
- Victims were consistently the **"top 5-10% performing officers"** who specifically worked against Russian interests
- This suggests **targeted neutralization** rather than random harassment
- Strategic goal: Remove the most effective U.S. officers through traumatic brain injury

## Weaknesses in Evidence

1. **Correlation ≠ causation** — presence near AHI sites doesn't prove responsibility
2. **Intelligence gaps** — no intercepts, no defector testimony, no weapons seizure (prior to 2026 device recovery)
3. **Private denials** — U.S. intelligence collection shows credible adversaries denying involvement
4. **ODNI assessment** — 7 agencies found no compelling evidence tying foreign actor
5. **Alternative explanations** — MPI, environmental factors could explain symptoms without Russian involvement

## Strengths in Evidence

1. **Temporal pattern** — incidents declined after CIA Director Burns warned Russian counterparts (2021-2022)
2. **Multiple corroborating sources** — 60 Minutes, The Insider, Der Spiegel independent investigations
3. **Documentary evidence** — Grozev found accounting document ("the receipt") for acoustic weapons work
4. **Specific individual identification** — Avarionov identified by victim at scene
5. **Technical capability** — technologies confirmed extant and fieldable by 2016
6. **Historical precedent** — Moscow Signal establishes pattern of Russian directed energy operations
7. **Domestic attacks** — Incidents on U.S. soil (Florida, California, near White House)
8. **Targeted victim selection** — Top performers who worked against Russia specifically

## Assessment

The evidence has grown significantly with the 60 Minutes investigation. The accounting document linking Unit 29155 to acoustic weapons work is the closest thing to **documentary proof** yet obtained. Combined with:
- Victim identification of specific individuals at attack scenes
- The clear targeting pattern (top 5-10% performers against Russia)
- Domestic attacks on U.S. soil
- The 2026 recovery of a backpack-sized RF device

The case against Unit 29155 is now the **strongest single attribution theory** for AHIs, though official U.S. intelligence assessments remain non-committal.
