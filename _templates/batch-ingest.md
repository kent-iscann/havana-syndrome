## SOURCE SUMMARIES (entries/summaries/)

### 1. arb-report-2018
The heavily redacted State Department Accountability Review Board (ARB) report, dated June 7 2018, marked SECRET/NOFORN. Released via FOIA to the National Security Archive. It found "systemic disorganization", lack of senior leadership, and failure to follow established procedures. The CIA withdrew its Havana station and informed State in Sep 2017. The Tillerson administration reduced embassy staff by 60% in Sep 2017 without a formal risk/benefit analysis. The ARB confirmed similar health incidents in Guangzhou (China), Tashkent, and one other redacted country. The State Department blocked CDC research for nearly a year citing privacy concerns.

### 2. nsarchive-secrets-havana-syndrome-2021
National Security Archive (Peter Kornbluh) publication from Feb 10 2021. First publication of declassified ARB report. Highlights: Tillerson State Department failed to conduct risk/benefit assessment before reducing embassy staff. CIA station closure confirmed. FBI/CIA excessive secrecy noted. Timeline from late 2016 (first episodes) to June 2018 (ARB submission). Excerpts: "The mechanism for the cause of the injuries is currently unknown." "The Department of State's response to these incidents was characterized by a lack of senior leadership, ineffective communications, and systemic disorganization."

### 3. odni-assessment-2023-2025
ODNI Annual Threat Assessment / IC assessment. 2023: seven agencies concluded foreign adversary involvement "very unlikely," no credible evidence tying foreign actor to specific AHI events. 2025 update: majority (5 of 7 agencies) maintained "very unlikely" stance, but two agencies shifted — one assessing "roughly even chance" a foreign country used a novel weapon, another assessing "roughly even chance" a foreign actor *possesses* such capability. Low confidence on minority assessments. IC collection efforts haven't produced compelling intelligence linking a foreign actor to specific events. Intelligence shows credible adversaries expressing surprise/denying involvement in private communications.

### 4. cia-interim-report-2022
CIA interim report (Feb 2022) found it "unlikely" a hostile foreign power is conducting a global campaign of AHIs. Many cases explained by environmental factors or pre-existing medical conditions. The assessment was controversial among victims who felt gaslighted. CIA Deputy Director David Cohen reportedly warned Russian counterparts of consequences if attacks continued.

### 5. cnn-new-intelligence-2025
CNN Politics article (Jan 10 2025) covering the ODNI summary report split. Majority view still skeptical of foreign involvement. Two agencies (reportedly NSA as one) assessed "roughly even chance." Political friction between IC, Congress, and victims. Victims feel gaslighted and believe IC is ignoring evidence pointing to Russia. House Intelligence Committee issued report finding "increasingly likely" a foreign adversary was responsible. White House directed agencies to prioritize investigations and ensure personnel receive medical care.

### 6. senate-intel-committee-report-2024
Declassified US Senate Intelligence Committee report (18 pages, released Dec 27 2024). Found CIA mishandled AHI cases: "delayed, denied or pre-conditioned care." Nearly 100 CIA-affiliated individuals who reported AHIs faced communication failures. Medical providers noted "significant moral injury" affecting patient recovery. Only 21% of CIA applicants approved for workers' comp vs 67% at other agencies. CIA contested claims more aggressively. Report warns "many unanswered questions" due to research gaps. Adversaries likely developing directed energy tech that may plausibly explain some symptoms.

### 7. giordano-ndu-paper-2022
"Anomalous Health Incidents of the Havana Syndrome: Implications and Lessons for Global Biosecurity and Defense" by James Giordano and Diane DiEuliis, published at National Defense University Digital Commons (2022). Established that ~2 dozen original Havana 2016 cases are regarded as verified physical injury. Directed energy device remains valid possibility. State of science and technology sufficiently advanced to produce and operationally employ such devices. ODNI focused on subset of priority cases.

### 8. giordano-ndm-2024
"Directed Energy Remains Key Suspect Behind Havana Syndrome," by Dr. James Giordano, National Defense Magazine, April 4 2024. Giordano was lead forensic evaluator for original 2016 cases. Primary suspects: ultrasonic stimulation and pulsed microwaves. "Frye Effect" (auditory sensations from microwave pulses) cited. Technologies were "extant, scalable, fieldable, and in operational use" by 2016. NASEM findings corroborated. Both transatlantic and transpacific near-peer competitors developing directed energy. "Clear and present risk" requiring shift in political/intelligence/military posture.

### 9. fpri-history-behind-mystery-2024
"Havana Syndrome: The History Behind the Mystery" by Lewis Regenstein, Foreign Policy Research Institute, April 1 2024. ~1,500 Americans reported AHIs. Connects to "Moscow Signal" (1953-1970s Soviet microwave irradiation). Historical precedents: Kissinger protest 1975, Nixon incident 1959. Russian Unit 29155 link from 60 Minutes/The Insider/Der Spiegel joint report. VP Kamala Harris Vietnam trip delayed 2021 due to possible AHI. Mobile phone tracking placed Russian intelligence near CIA targets at attack sites. CIA Director Burns warned Russian counterparts 2021-2022.

### 10. nih-jama-studies-2024
NIH clinical studies published in JAMA (March 18 2024). Five-year study of ~80 current/former officials. Found NO significant differences in brain structure, function, or biomarkers between AHI patients and control groups. Failed to replicate 2018 UPenn findings. Note: studies were subsequently shuttered after internal investigation revealed some participants were coerced to join. NIH did find higher rates of imbalance, fatigue, PTSD, and depression among AHI reporters.

## ENTITY PAGES (entries/entities/)

The CIA withdrew its Havana station and personnel in Sep 2017 after two TDY officers fell ill at Hotel Nacional. CIA interim report (Feb 2022) concluded foreign adversary involvement "unlikely" — controversial among victims. ### 12. state-department
U.S. State Department, the diplomatic arm responsible for embassy operations and personnel welfare. The ARB (June 2018) found "systemic disorganization" in State's response. Under Secretary Tillerson, the department reduced Havana embassy staff 60% without formal risk/benefit analysis. State initially blocked CDC research for nearly a year citing privacy concerns. Secretary Pompeo received the classified ARB report in June 2018. State Department designated AHIs as a condition qualifying for HAVANA Act compensation.

### 13. gru-unit-29155
GRU Unit 29155 is a Russian military intelligence special operations unit implicated in a 2024 joint investigation (60 Minutes, The Insider, Der Spiegel) into AHI incidents. Linked to "non-lethal acoustic weapons" awards. Members identified at or near AHI incident locations including Poland, Georgia, Australia, and Taiwan. Albert Averyanov (son of GRU deputy chief) identified by a victim outside her home in Georgia during an attack. The unit is also known for involvement in the Salisbury poisoning and other covert operations.

### 14. fsb

Mobile phone tracking data placed FSB/GRU officers in close physical proximity to CIA targets in Poland, Georgia, Australia, and Taiwan at the time of AHI attacks. Russia has been developing "psychotronic" weapons designed to attack the central nervous system, announced in 2012 by the Defense Minister. ### 15. odni

### 16..nih

### 18. guangzhou-consulate

### 19. vienna-embassy

## CONCEPT PAGES (entries/concepts/)

### 22. directed-energy-weapons
Weapons that direct focused energy against a target. In the AHI context, includes:
- **Pulsed RF/Microwave**: Directed electromagnetic energy pulses that can penetrate structures and interact with body tissues
- **Ultrasonic/Acoustic**: Directional sound at frequencies above human hearing
- **Laser**: Pulsed infrared lasers creating optoacoustic effects in the inner ear
Directed energy can be portable, backpack-sized, silent, and non-heating. Technologies were "extant, scalable, fieldable, and in operational use" by 2016 (Giordano). Russia has announced development of "psychotronic" weapons. U.S. Homeland Security reportedly acquired a backpack-sized pulsed RF device in 2026.

### 23. fry-effect
Also spelled "Frey Effect." Discovered by Allan H. Frey in 1960s. The phenomenon where humans can perceive pulsed RF/microwave energy as sounds (buzzing, chirping, clicking) — auditory sensations from microwave pulses. This is the leading candidate to explain the "strange noise" reported by AHI victims before symptom onset.

### 24. ultrasonic-weapons
Directional acoustic or ultrasound devices capable of producing physical effects at a distance. Giordano's 2017 forensic evaluation identified ultrasonic stimulation as a high-probability cause alongside pulsed microwaves. JASON advisory group suggested the infamous "Havana sound" recordings might be the Indies short-tailed cricket.

### 25. mass-psychogenic-illness
A psychological/social mechanism where genuine physical symptoms spread among groups through suggestion, stress, or moral panic. Proposed by the FBI Behavioral Analysis Unit (2018) and researchers like Bartholomew & Baloh. They suggest the "nocebo effect" — stress and fear manifest as authentic physical symptoms. The Cuban Academy of Sciences favored this explanation in 2021. Many victims and researchers vehemently reject this characterization.

### 26. indies-short-tailed-cricket
Identification of the insect species whose calls match audio recordings made by Havana AHI victims. The JASON advisory group (2018) concluded the "strange sounds" were most likely this cricket. Many AHI victims and researchers dispute this as the sole explanation, noting the sounds were reported in locations where this cricket doesn't exist.

### 27. optoacoustic-effect
Proposed in 2026 by a Czech Academy of Sciences fellow. The mechanism whereby pulsed infrared lasers generate acoustic effects in tissue (particularly the inner ear). Portability: infrared lasers can be small, silent, and don't create heat. Proposed as a portable, covert alternative to microwave-based directed energy for causing AHI-like symptoms.

### 28. form-244
Department of Defense acute assessment protocol for Anomalous Health Incident reporting. Established to streamline the medical evaluation process for active-duty personnel. A 2024 GAO report found patients were often confused by the care-seeking process despite Form 244, and recommended streamlining.

### 29. havana-syndrome-symptoms
Clinical presentation of AHI victims:
**Acute onset:** Blurred vision, light sensitivity, intense head pressure/vibration, ear pain, tinnitus, dizziness, nausea, vertigo, disorientation, spatial perceptual abnormalities, perceived loud screeching/chirping/clicking sounds.
**Chronic/long-term:** Cognitive impairment ("brain fog"), balance issues, insomnia, persistent headaches, diminished memory (short, intermediate, long-term), impaired motor abilities, neurocognitive abnormalities (vigilance, task focus), fatigue.
NASEM 2020 found "a constellation of acute clinical signs and symptoms with directional locational specific features that was distinctive to its knowledge. This constellation of clinical features is unlike any disorder in neurological or general medical literature."
NIH 2024 found no MRI-detectable brain injury biomarkers but noted higher rates of imbalance, fatigue, PTSD, depression.

## ANALYSIS PAGES (entries/analysis/)

### 30. moscow-signal-to-havana-syndrome
The Moscow Signal (1953-1979) involved the Soviet Union irradiating the U.S. Embassy in Moscow with microwaves — for up to 16 hours daily, 7 days per week. This was done for bugging or surveillance-disruption purposes. Three U.S. Ambassadors to Moscow (Bohlen, Thompson, Stoessel) died of cancer at relatively early ages. The Nixon incident (1959): Secret Service detected high radiation in VP Nixon's Moscow residence, covered up for decades. Kissinger privately pressured Soviet Ambassador Dobrynin in 1975 to "turn off the beam." This historical precedent strongly suggests Havana Syndrome may be an evolution of Soviet/Russian directed energy operations rather than a new phenomenon. The key difference: Cold War Moscow Signal was for surveillance, Havana Syndrome appears to be anti-personnel.

### 31. directed-energy-evidence-debate
The central debate over Havana Syndrome causation has two camps:

**Directed Energy Camp:**
- NASEM 2020: Pulsed RF energy "most plausible"
- Giordano forensic evaluation: ultrasonic and pulsed microwave high probability
- DNI Scientific Panel 2022: Pulsed electromagnetic energy and ultrasound "plausible"
- Technologies were extant by 2016
- GRU Unit 29155 members found at AHI locations

**Skeptical Camp:**

- ODNI 2023: 7 agencies, no credible evidence tying foreign actor
- NIH 2024: No detectable brain injury biomarkers
- JASON report: Cricket sounds

**Status (Jan 2025):** Majority "very unlikely" foreign involvement. Two agencies shifted to "roughly even chance" — first crack in consensus.
